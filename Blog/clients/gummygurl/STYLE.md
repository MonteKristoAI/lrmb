# GummyGurl -- Writing Style Guide

## Voice: Knowledgeable friend who happens to know a lot about hemp products

### Tone Dimensions
- Funny <-> Serious: 4/10 (mostly informative, occasional light humor)
- Formal <-> Casual: 7/10 (casual, approachable, but not sloppy)
- Respectful <-> Irreverent: 4/10 (respectful of reader's intelligence)
- Enthusiastic <-> Matter-of-fact: 6/10 (genuinely enthusiastic about products, grounded in facts)

### Voice Characteristics
- Talks like someone who has personally tried every product on the shelf
- Uses plain language for cannabinoid science, never talks down
- Leads with what the reader actually wants to know (dosing, effects, legality)
- Drops specific product names and prices naturally, like a recommendation from a friend
- Addresses legal concerns directly without hedging or legal-speak
- Treats the reader as an adult making an informed choice

### Sentence Style
- Average sentence length: 14-18 words
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Contractions: yes, always ("you'll", "it's", "we've")
- Mix of punchy 1-sentence paragraphs with longer explanatory ones
- Questions in body text to break up dense sections

### Regional Vernacular / Industry Terms
- "Edibles" not "cannabis-infused products"
- "Gummies" not "gummy supplements"
- "Delta-8", "delta-9", "THCA" (always hyphenated/capitalized correctly)
- "Hemp-derived" not "hemp-based"
- "Lab-tested" not "laboratory verified"
- "COA" (Certificate of Analysis) -- define on first use per post
- "Farm Bill compliant" -- reference 2018 Farm Bill
- "Milligrams per serving" not "dosage units"
- Strain names capitalized: "Purple Haze", "Sour Cookies"

## Do Not Use (Banned)

### Banned Words (AI Detection Risk)
delve, tapestry, nuanced, realm, landscape, multifaceted, pivotal, utilize, facilitate, elucidate, comprehensive (as filler), robust, seamless, cutting-edge, transformative, innovative (generic), dynamic, agile, game-changer, unlock (metaphorical), revolutionize, crucial, moreover, furthermore, in conclusion, it's worth noting, it's important to note, dive deep, embark, plethora, myriad, elevate, uncover, unveil, navigate (metaphorical), empower, synergy, paradigm

### Banned Openings
- "In today's fast-paced/modern/evolving world..."
- "Let's dive into..."
- "Whether you're a beginner or experienced..."
- "This is something we all..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Have you ever wondered..."

### Industry-Specific Bans
- Never claim medical benefits ("cures", "treats", "heals")
- Never guarantee specific effects ("will make you feel...")
- No dosing advice that sounds like a prescription
- No underage references or imagery
- No comparative health claims vs pharmaceuticals

## Do Use (Human Signals)

### Experience Markers
- "We tested the Magnum gummies at 50mg and the onset was about 45 minutes"
- "Our CBN Sleep gummy is the one customers reorder most"
- "The Sour Cookies THCA strain has a terpene profile that leans citrus"
- "At $24.99 for 500mg, the Good Karma gummies are our best value per milligram"
- "We've shipped to 38 states since launching last year"

### Seamus Voice Corpus (from real customer emails)

These patterns come from 5 real customer emails written by Seamus McKeon. Use them to calibrate tone, pacing, and word choice in all GummyGurl blog content.

**Opening style:** Casual "Hey," - never "Dear" or "Hello" or formal greetings.

**Sign-off:** "-- Gummy Gurl" (brand name, not personal name).

**Sentence rhythm:** Short, punchy, declarative. One idea per sentence.
- "Nothing sits around here."
- "We don't like delays either."
- "Bigger dose, longer ride. Not for beginners."

**Product recommendations:** Direct name drops without preamble or buildup. No "you might want to consider" - just tell them what to get.
- "Go with Magnum. Bigger dose, longer ride. Not for beginners."
- Assumes the reader knows what they want. Gives a clear pick, then explains why in plain terms.

**Owning mistakes:** Honest, direct, no corporate deflection. Acknowledges the problem and names what it should have been.
- "Sorry about that - that's not the experience we want anyone to have."

**Anti-corporate positioning:** Calls out the noise in the industry. Draws a clear line between GummyGurl and generic brands.
- "There's a lot of noise in this space right now. Same flavors. Same claims. Same vague labeling. We're not doing that."

**Clarity-focused selling:** Leads with specifics, not adjectives. No "premium quality" - instead, name what makes it different.
- "Exact dosing, no guesswork. Clean formulas. Lab-tested and easy to verify."

**Conversational closes:** Invites the reader to just reply. Low friction, no forms, no funnels.
- "If you're not sure what direction to go, just reply and we'll point you the right way."

**Voice rules derived from corpus:**
1. Never open with a formal greeting in blog CTAs or product callouts - match the "Hey," energy
2. Keep product recommendation sentences under 10 words where possible
3. When addressing a problem or complaint, lead with the apology, then state what should have happened
4. Position GummyGurl against "the industry" not against named competitors
5. Sell through clarity (dosing, testing, ingredients) not through hype words

### Specificity Markers
- Real product names (FUBAR Gummies, Midnight Bluezzz, Puckerz)
- Real prices ($24.99, $34.99, $49.99)
- Real milligram counts (500mg, 1000mg, 5000mg)
- Real strain names (Purple Haze, Space Runtz, Apple Fritter)
- COA batch numbers where relevant
- Specific cannabinoid ratios

## Content Formatting (4 Template Types)

| Template | Key Takeaways | FAQ | Words | Opens With |
|----------|:---:|:---:|-------|-----------|
| A: Long-form Guide | Yes | Yes (5+) | 2,500-3,500 | Hook stat + takeaways |
| B: Short Direct Answer | No | No | 1,200-1,800 | Direct answer in first line |
| C: Story-led | No | Yes (3-4) | 1,800-2,500 | Scenario/persona hook |
| D: Data Piece | Yes | No | 1,500-2,200 | Key stat + comparison table |

**Variation rule:** Never 3+ consecutive posts with same template. Rotate A-B-C-D.

These products are intended for adults 21 and older. Consult a healthcare professional before using any cannabinoid products, especially if you take prescription medications.

## Product Linking Rules

- Link to gummygurl.com product pages (not external retailer sites)
- Include price in parentheses on first mention
- Use UTM parameters: `?utm_source=blog&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign={slug}`
- Max 3 product links per post (not a catalog, it's editorial)
