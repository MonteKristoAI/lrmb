# GummyGurl Perfect Post Template

*Source: L100 critic recommendation. Highest CVR template for hemp e-commerce.*

## Template: "Product Decision Post"

### Structure

**TITLE**: [Comparison/Decision keyword] + [Year]
Example: "Delta-8 Chocolate vs Gummies: Which Hits Better in 2026?"

**SECTION 1: Direct Answer (50 words)**
Start with the verdict. "Gummies hit faster (30-45 min) but chocolates last longer (6-8 hours). For most people, gummies win on convenience. Here's why."

**SECTION 2: Key Takeaways Box**
5 bullets. 2+ contain specific numbers from COA data or published research. Each bullet is a self-contained citable passage.

**SECTION 3: Comparison Table**
`<thead>` required. Columns: Feature | Option A | Option B | Winner. Rows: onset time, duration, dosage precision, taste, price per mg, lab testing, portability. All data from GummyGurl products with real prices and real mg counts.

**SECTION 4: "We Tested Both" (E-E-A-T section)**
200 words. Specific product names (FUBAR Gummies $34.99 vs Delta-8 Chocolate Bar $29.99). Specific test conditions. Specific observations. Photo of both products with COA visible. This section separates GummyGurl from competitors who write comparison posts from spec sheets they found online.

**SECTION 5: Who Should Pick What**
3 buyer scenarios mapped to specific products. Each scenario ends with a product link (UTM tagged, max 3 links total).

**SECTION 6: The November 2026 Factor (required on all THC posts)**
50-word acknowledgment of upcoming regulation. Links to hub regulatory post. Drives email signup: "Get notified when hemp law changes."

**SECTION 7: FAQ (5 pairs, schema-ready)**
Sourced from People Also Ask. Each answer 80-120 words, self-contained.

**FOOTER**: Compliance disclaimer + author bio + 4 further reading links.

### Why This Template Converts

1. **Direct answer first** -- captures AI citation and featured snippets
2. **Comparison table** -- scannable, shareable, AI-citable (+47% citation rate with thead)
3. **"We Tested" section** -- E-E-A-T proof that no AI-only competitor can replicate
4. **Buyer scenarios** -- matches reader to specific product (reduces decision paralysis)
5. **November 2026 urgency** -- drives email capture on every THC post
6. **Short and dense** -- 1,800-2,200 words is the sweet spot for decision-stage content (not 3,500 word walls)
