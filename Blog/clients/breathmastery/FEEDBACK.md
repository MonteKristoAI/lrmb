## [2026-03-28] — Inferred from full blog content analysis (103 posts, 1976–2026)

Based on analysis of all 103 published blog posts and the complete site, the following patterns represent what this client consistently publishes and approves:

### Structure & Format
- Every post must include **at least one practice exercise** the reader can do immediately — not appended at the end, but embedded in the flow
- **SEO/Admin posts** (technique explainers, anxiety posts, beginner guides) must include a **FAQ section** with 3–5 Q&A pairs
- **Dan's personal posts** do not require a FAQ — the flowing prose with embedded exercises is the format
- Always include a **safety note** in any post featuring powerful, rapid, or extended breathing techniques (connected breathing, Bhastrika, Holotropic-style, extended breath holds)
- Technique posts should always include: what the technique is, how to do it step by step, when to practice it, and who should avoid it

### Voice & Tone
- **Never use intensity or force language** — Dan's brand is explicitly anti-intensity: "regulation beats intensity," "gentleness usually takes you further than intensity"
- Every post should feel like **an invitation**, not an instruction or a command
- Science and spirituality must **coexist** — never strip the spiritual dimension from functional posts or add woo-woo to posts that should be grounded in physiology
- Dan's personal posts always close with: **"Good luck in your practice, and many blessings on your path."** — use this exact phrase, never paraphrase it
- Dan's personal posts are signed: **Dan (Guchu Ram Singh)** — use this name format if author attribution is needed

### Authority & Credibility
- Always reference **Dan's lineage** at least once per post — who trained him, who he trained, the 300,000+ people in 73 countries. This is not optional; it is the brand's credibility anchor.
- When citing a technique by name, **attribute it correctly** — Rebirthing to Leonard Orr, Holotropic to Stan Grof, Vivation to Jim Leonard, Coherent Breathing to Stephen Elliott (see BRAND.md table)
- **Tony Robbins is a personal client** who wrote the foreword to *Just Breathe* — mention this when appropriate (not in every post, but as a credibility note in authority-building content)

### The Two Pillars
- The terms **Breath Awareness** and **Conscious Breathing** are the Two Pillars and the brand's central framework — reference these in most posts, and always define them the same way:
  - Breath Awareness = passive, observing without controlling
  - Conscious Breathing = active, directing with intention
- The formula **"Open and expand. Relax and let go."** is the core mantra — use in posts where the practice is featured

### CTA Patterns
- CTAs are **always invitational** — never "sign up now," "buy today," "don't miss out"
- Primary CTA hierarchy (match to post type):
  1. Beginner/entry posts → **21-Day Breathing Training** (free opt-in)
  2. Deep practice posts → **Legacy Collection** ($397) or **Inner Circle** ($399/year)
  3. Professional/career posts → **Practitioner Certification Program** ($7,560)
  4. Event/community posts → **Breathfest** or upcoming live events
- The Practitioner Certification is the **highest-value offer** — give it at least one mention per month in the content calendar

### What Every Post Should Do
- Open with the **benefit, problem, or human experience** — never a definition or a dictionary opening
- Give the reader **something to practice** — breathwork is experiential, not theoretical
- Honor **both the beginner and the practitioner** — write so a first-timer can follow along and an advanced practitioner finds depth
- Include a **path forward** — where can the reader go next? (internal link, course mention, event)

### What to Avoid (Observed from Blog Analysis)
- Do not write posts that are purely theoretical with no practice guidance
- Do not use generic "wellness" language without Dan's specific angle
- Do not start with: "Breathing is something we all do automatically" — Dan's blog has never opened this way
- Do not use intensity/achievement framing around breathwork outcomes
- Do not omit safety notes on powerful techniques — Dan consistently includes them even in short posts
