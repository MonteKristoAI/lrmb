Use this document during the onboarding call with Chris. Two sections: Voice Corpus (for blog writing quality) and Blog Planning (for content strategy).

## Part A: Voice Corpus (30 min recorded call)

**Purpose:** We need to capture how Chris actually talks so the blog sounds like HIM, not like a marketing agency. Record this call and transcribe it.

### Questions:

1. **"Walk me through your last three installations. What happened? What was tricky? What did the homeowner ask?"**
   - *Listen for: his vocabulary, how he describes products, what details he naturally mentions*

2. **"What's the number one mistake people make when choosing shutters or blinds?"**
   - *Listen for: how he explains things to non-experts, his teaching voice*

3. **"If someone calls you tomorrow and says they're building a new home in Wagga, what do you tell them?"**
   - *Listen for: his sales/consultation approach, what he recommends first*

4. **"What do you think about PVC vs basswood? What do you actually recommend and why?"**
   - *Listen for: honest product opinions, specific product names he uses*

5. **"Which products do you personally enjoy installing the most? Why?"**
   - *Listen for: passion points, expertise areas*

6. **"What's the weirdest or trickiest window you've ever had to fit?"**
   - *Listen for: storytelling ability, technical problem-solving*

7. **"How do you explain the difference between your products and what someone could buy at Bunnings?"**
   - *Listen for: value proposition in his own words, competitive positioning*

8. **"What do customers complain about most with other companies?"**
   - *Listen for: competitor weaknesses he's aware of, his differentiators*

9. **"When a customer asks you 'how much will this cost?' what do you say?"**
   - *Listen for: how he handles pricing conversations, comfort level with publishing prices*

10. **"What's the busiest time of year for you? When's it quiet?"**
    - *Listen for: seasonal patterns, capacity constraints*

### What to Extract from the Call:
- [ ] Words and phrases Chris uses repeatedly
- [ ] How he refers to products (short names vs full names)
- [ ] His sentence length and rhythm (short and direct? or detailed?)
- [ ] Stories and anecdotes he naturally tells
- [ ] Topics he gets passionate about
- [ ] How he explains technical concepts to homeowners
- [ ] His honest product opinions and recommendations

## Part B: Blog Planning Questions

**Purpose:** Get Chris's input on content priorities, pricing transparency, and photo availability. These answers directly shape which blog posts we write first.

### Content Priorities:

1. **"Which products are your highest margin? What do you most want to sell more of?"**
   - *This determines which clusters we prioritize*

2. **"What questions do customers ask you the most? Give me your top 5."**
   - *These become our first blog posts*

3. **"What's the one thing you wish every customer knew before they called you?"**
   - *This becomes a high-priority educational post*

### Pricing Transparency:

4. **"We want to publish real installed price ranges on the blog. Not your cost price, but what homeowners actually pay. Are you comfortable with that?"**
   - YES → We publish price ranges (e.g., "$300-$600/m2 installed"). This is our #1 competitive advantage, no one else in the Riverina does this.
   - HESITANT → We publish general ranges without exact per-product breakdowns.
   - NO → We skip pricing posts (significantly weakens the strategy).

5. **"Are you OK with us saying things like 'Forte Select starts at $X installed' using your actual CWGlobal product names?"**
   - *Naming specific products signals dealer expertise*

### Visual Content:

6. **"Do you have any before/after photos from recent jobs sitting on your phone right now?"**
   - *If yes: get them transferred immediately. These are our first case study photos.*

7. **"Would you be willing to take 3-5 quick photos after each installation? Just pull out your phone, snap a few shots of the finished result?"**
   - *Gauge enthusiasm level. If low, we rely on the 5-layer photo backup system.*

8. **"Can we come visit you on a job site once a quarter to take professional photos and a quick video?"**
   - *Even one visit produces 3-4 case study photo sets*

### Competitors & Market:

9. **"Who do you lose jobs to? Apollo? Watson? Online companies?"**
   - *Identifies competitive angles for content*

10. **"Are there any builders in the area you work with regularly who might be up for a partnership? Interior designers?"**
    - *Content partnerships = backlinks = SEO authority*

11. **"Do you have a Facebook page? Instagram? Who manages it?"**
    - *Blog content gets repurposed to social*

### Blog Operations:

12. **"We'll write everything. You just need to review posts before they go live. Is email the best way to send them to you? Or would you prefer a shared Google Doc?"**
    - *Sets the approval workflow*

13. **"If we send you a post to review and you're busy, is it OK to publish after 48 hours without hearing back?"**
    - *Establishes the auto-publish rule for non-case-study content*

14. **"Are there any topics you feel strongly should be covered first? Or topics you'd rather we avoid?"**
    - *Respects Chris's business judgement*
