# AiiACo Buyer Personas (4 Deep Profiles)

**Purpose**: give writers and AI models concrete reader targets so every post speaks to a specific person, not a generic "audience". Each persona below has role, background, tech stack, pain points in their own words, objections, buying trigger, content preferences, and media consumption.

Every AiiACo post must identify which of the 4 personas it primarily targets. Write as if that person is reading it.

---

## Persona A: Jeff Martinelli, Managing Broker of a 40-Agent Real Estate Brokerage

### Snapshot

- **Name**: Jeff Martinelli (fictional composite based on 3 real AiiACo prospects)
- **Title**: Managing Broker / Owner
- **Company size**: 40 agents, 2 offices
- **Market**: Secondary US metro (400k population)
- **Revenue**: $8M GCI, $2M brokerage retention
- **Age**: 47
- **Background**: 22 years in real estate. Started as an agent, built his team, bought out his mentor in 2018. Managing broker since then.

### Tech stack

- CRM: Follow Up Boss (Pro plan, $500/year/agent)
- Website: IDX from iHomeFinder, custom WordPress theme
- Lead sources: Zillow Premier Agent ($6k/month), Realtor.com ($2k/month), Google Ads ($4k/month), referrals (35% of volume)
- Transaction management: Dotloop
- Reporting: spreadsheets + Follow Up Boss native reports
- Communication: Slack for agent team, Gmail for clients
- Social: Instagram, Facebook, YouTube (rare)

### What keeps Jeff up at night

Quotes reconstructed from Reddit r/realtors patterns + AiiACo prospect calls:

> "My top 5 agents produce 60% of the revenue. The other 35 produce the rest and eat all my overhead. I cannot keep feeding leads into agents who do not convert."

> "I paid for a chatbot last year that was supposed to qualify leads. It made leads worse. Half my agents stopped using Follow Up Boss entirely."

> "I cannot afford McKinsey. I do not want another vendor demo where they show me 20 slides. I want someone to tell me what Follow Up Boss plus AI can actually do for my 40 agents this quarter."

> "The worst part of my month is the pipeline review. I stare at the Follow Up Boss dashboard and ask my agents why their deals stopped moving. Half the time they do not know. The other half of the time they did not update the stage."

### Jeff's objections (pre-empt in content)

1. "We tried a chatbot and it was a disaster." → Counter: chatbots are not AI integration. Layer integration is different.
2. "I cannot afford a consulting engagement over $50k." → Counter: first module ships in 3-5 weeks at $20-30k. Start small.
3. "My agents will not adopt new tools." → Counter: they keep using Follow Up Boss as-is. The AI layer runs in the background.
4. "What about Fair Housing Act compliance?" → Counter: every engagement includes FHA compliance layer. Explicit in the contract.
5. "How do I know it will work for my brokerage?" → Counter: share the 40-agent case reference (when available), offer a diagnostic call.

### Jeff's buying trigger

- Q1 planning cycle (January, when he reviews agent productivity)
- Loss of a top producer (when his revenue concentration risk becomes painful)
- Zillow cost increase (when his paid-lead ROI drops)
- New compliance letter from his state real estate commission

### Jeff reads

- Inman (daily email digest)
- HousingWire (weekly)
- Tom Ferry YouTube (monthly)
- The Close blog (occasional)
- LinkedIn (Nemr's network)

### Content Jeff will read

- Operator walkthroughs with specific CRM mentions
- Pricing transparency
- Case studies with real numbers
- Compliance breakdowns
- Opinion posts (he likes contrarian takes)

### Content Jeff will NOT read

- Generic "AI is transforming real estate" thought leadership
- Sponsored vendor content
- Academic papers
- Long philosophical pieces

### Posts primarily targeting Jeff

Post 2, 3, 4, 9, 13, 15, 18, 23, 25, 26, 27, 32, 33, 34, 38, 41 (all Cluster 2 real estate)

### Jeff's ideal post length and format

- 1800-2400 words
- TL;DR bullets at top
- At least 3 specific CRM references
- FAQ section he can skim
- Closing paragraph with a dollar range

## Persona B: Rachel Chen, VP of Originations at a Regional Mortgage Lender

### Snapshot

- **Name**: Rachel Chen (fictional composite)
- **Title**: VP of Originations
- **Company size**: 120 LOs, $2B annual volume
- **Market**: 6 states in the Northeast US
- **Age**: 43
- **Background**: 18 years in mortgage. Started as an LO, moved to ops management. Has a reputation internally for pushing technology adoption.

### Tech stack

- LOS: Encompass by ICE Mortgage Technology (enterprise tier)
- POS: Floify + custom Salesforce
- Pricing engine: Optimal Blue
- Document management: Box + Encompass
- CRM: Total Expert (cross-selling tool)
- Marketing: HubSpot + Homebot for LO-level outreach
- Communication: Slack + Teams
- BI: Power BI + Tableau

### Rachel's pain

> "Our LOs spend 30 percent of their day on coordination work that the platform should handle. Encompass has 11 tabs, Floify has 8. Half the time LOs forget to check one. We lose deals in the cracks."

> "I get pitched by 3 AI vendors per week. They all promise the same thing. None of them have an Encompass integration that actually works end-to-end. They say 'we use the API' but they do not write back to the custom fields we use."

> "My CFO wants to know the ROI before we commit. I do not have a number I can defend. Every AI vendor says 'ROI in 90 days' but will not say what the ROI is actually measured against."

> "The dormant database sits at 28,000 past borrowers. Our LOs refi 200 of them per year because they remember the relationships. The other 27,800 do nothing. That is the biggest reactivation opportunity in my org and nobody is working on it."

### Rachel's objections

1. "Our IT team will not let us use OpenAI because of data compliance." → Counter: AiiACo deploys models via Azure OpenAI or private endpoints. SOC2 + HIPAA-lite compliant.
2. "What about TRID and QM?" → Counter: every AI-generated LO communication runs through a compliance filter before sending. Legal review on all templates.
3. "We tried LoanOfficer.ai and it was too narrow." → Counter: that is a single-product vendor. AiiACo is an integrator, not a vendor.
4. "How do I get budget approval?" → Counter: first module (dormant reactivation) has a clean ROI story. Start there. Expand on success.

### Rachel's buying trigger

- CFO request for AI budget line item
- Churn of a top LO (loss of relationship portfolio)
- New CFPB guidance on AI (regulatory pressure)
- Refi wave (when LOs are overwhelmed and dormant reactivation becomes urgent)

### Rachel reads

- HousingWire Mortgage (daily)
- National Mortgage Professional (weekly)
- MBA policy briefs (monthly)
- StratmorGroup insights (quarterly)
- MPA Magazine (weekly)
- Her state MBA chapter newsletter

### Posts primarily targeting Rachel

Post 5, 10, 16, 29, 35, 48 (Cluster 3 mortgage)

### Rachel's ideal post length and format

- 2400-3000 words
- Compliance callouts required
- At least 2 Encompass-specific references
- ROI numbers with methodology
- Executive-summary version she can forward to CFO

## Persona C: Priya Venkat, Operations Director at a Vacation Rental Management Company

### Snapshot

- **Name**: Priya Venkat (fictional composite)
- **Title**: Operations Director
- **Company size**: 180 managed units, 14 employees
- **Market**: Coastal vacation rental market in the US Southeast
- **Age**: 38
- **Background**: 12 years in hospitality, 6 in vacation rental. Handles everything operations-adjacent: guest communications, housekeeping coordination, maintenance scheduling, dynamic pricing oversight.

### Tech stack

- PMS: Guesty (Pro plan)
- Channel manager: Guesty native
- Pricing: PriceLabs + manual overrides
- Cleaning: Turno + WhatsApp groups
- Guest messaging: Guesty Unified Inbox + sometimes ChatGPT for hard replies
- Payment: Stripe via Guesty
- Review management: manual (reads every review, responds personally)
- Accounting: QuickBooks Online + Guesty reports

### Priya's pain

> "Our guest messaging volume is 400 to 600 per day in peak season. Two of my team members do nothing but reply to messages. Half the messages are the same questions over and over. Where is the code. How does the AC work. What time is check-in."

> "Pricing is overmanaged. PriceLabs gives me suggestions, but the suggestions are dumb on special events. I override it for 20 percent of nights. Every override is a decision I make manually."

> "Guesty is adding AI features now. Some of them are good. Most of them are marketing. I cannot tell which. I do not want to bet my season on a feature that was announced at the user conference and then abandoned."

> "I love Guesty but I cannot rely on them for the specific things my operation needs. I need a custom AI layer that talks to Guesty but also talks to Turno and my owner portal and my maintenance ticket system."

### Priya's objections

1. "I already pay for Guesty. Why would I pay again for an AI layer?" → Counter: Guesty AI features are general-purpose. AiiACo builds your operation's specific flows.
2. "My data is too unique for AI to learn." → Counter: modern models fine-tune on small datasets (50-200 examples) per use case.
3. "What if the AI says something wrong to a guest?" → Counter: AI drafts, human reviews for first 30 days. Then auto-send on whitelisted templates.
4. "How do I measure if it is working?" → Counter: reply time, review score, number of escalations. Clear unit metrics.

### Priya's buying trigger

- Peak season prep (Feb-April)
- Addition of 25+ new units (threshold where messaging volume becomes unmanageable)
- Staff turnover (losing a messaging specialist)
- New Guesty feature that disappoints her

### Priya reads

- Rental Scale-Up (weekly)
- VRMA member newsletter (monthly)
- Hostaway blog (occasionally)
- Guesty blog (mostly reference)
- The Short Term Rental Show podcast (biweekly)

### Posts primarily targeting Priya

Post 12, 17, 22, 39, 47 (Cluster 4 vacation rental)

### Priya's ideal post length and format

- 2000-2600 words
- Guesty + Hostaway + PriceLabs references mandatory
- Specific messaging volume numbers
- Real case stories from other 100-300 unit operators
- 4-step or 5-step playbooks (she is a doer, not a strategist)

## Persona use in content

At the start of every post draft, the writer marks the primary persona:

```
<!-- PRIMARY_PERSONA: A | B | C | D -->
```

This is NOT part of the META block emitted to HTML. It is a writer comment for alignment. The writer reads the persona profile before writing and asks: would Jeff (or Rachel, or Priya, or Marcus) want to read this? Would they forward it? Would they book a consultation after reading it?

If the answer to those questions is no, the draft needs a voice adjustment before shipping.

## Persona-specific writing reminders

| When writing for | Do | Do not |
|------------------|-----|--------|
| Jeff (Persona A) | Name Follow Up Boss by name. Tell agent stories. Publish dollar ranges. | Write in corporate voice. Mention enterprise architecture. Hide pricing. |
| Rachel (Persona B) | Reference Encompass, Floify, Total Expert. Talk TRID/QM/CFPB. Publish CFO-ready ROI. | Hand-wave about "compliance". Avoid regulatory specificity. Skip ROI math. |
