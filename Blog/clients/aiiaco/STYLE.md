# AiiACo Blog Style Guide

**Voice owner**: Nemr Hallak, Founder and AI Systems Architect of AiiACo
**Calibration source**: Round 3 conversation log, `/manifesto` content, shipped posts `what-is-an-ai-revenue-system`, `how-to-integrate-ai-into-a-real-estate-crm`, `real-estate-brokerages-ai-mistakes`

## Voice pillars

### 1. Operator-to-operator
Every paragraph sounds like Nemr speaking to another operator, not a consultant selling to a stranger. Use first-person singular. Tell stories. Share opinions.

Do:
- "I have watched too many real estate brokerages spend six figures on AI tools and end up with nothing to show for it."
- "When I integrate AI into Encompass for a 40-LO team, the first thing I do is..."
- "In practice, the brokerages that win with AI share three habits."

Do not:
- "Organizations should consider how AI can transform their operations."
- "It is recommended that stakeholders evaluate the strategic implications."
- "Many companies fail to leverage the full potential of AI."

### 2. Specific, never generic
Every claim is anchored to a specific tool, API, number, or regulation. No abstractions.

Do:
- "Follow Up Boss exposes a REST API at `https://api.followupboss.com/v1/` with webhooks for lead events and sequence steps."
- "A 10,000-contact dormant database typically converts 15 to 25 percent to re-engaged conversations, and a single-digit percentage flows into active pipeline."
- "The EU AI Act Article 10 requires training data governance for high-risk systems - this applies to any real estate AI that scores leads."

Do not:
- "Modern CRMs support API integration."
- "A dormant database can generate significant pipeline."
- "AI regulation is becoming increasingly important."

### 3. Contrarian when warranted
Nemr has strong takes. The blog publishes them.

Do:
- "Do not rip out your CRM to install an AI-first platform. It throws away institutional memory."
- "AI SDR vendors sell a tool. An AI SDR is not a tool, it is a role - and a tool cannot own a role."
- "Dormant database reactivation is the single highest ROI use of AI in real estate, and brokerages skip it because it is boring."

Do not:
- Soft-pedal conclusions to sound balanced
- Hedge with "there is no one-size-fits-all answer"
- Use "might", "could", "may" when Nemr would say "will", "does", "is"

### 4. Transparent about pricing and timelines
Every post that touches engagement mechanics publishes real ranges, not marketing-speak "contact us for a custom quote".

Do:
- "First operational module ships in 3 to 5 weeks. Full five-component rollout takes 8 to 14 weeks."
- "A fractional AI team runs 15 to 25 hours per week for 8 to 14 weeks."
- "Dormant database reactivation engagements typically cost $15,000 to $45,000 depending on contact volume."

Do not:
- "Pricing depends on scope."
- "Timelines vary by engagement."
- "Contact us for a custom quote."

### 4b. Sanctioned outcome ranges (added 2026-04-11, Nemr approved)

These three directional claims are approved for use without source attribution. They are based on AiiACo engagement observations that Nemr has sanctioned as representative of what operators should expect. Use freely across posts where the context applies.

**The three ranges:**

1. **30 to 70 percent faster workflows** - apply to any efficiency or coordination claim (lead triage, listing content, follow-up, admin tasks)
2. **2 to 3x conversion lift** - apply to any pipeline or funnel claim (lead-to-meeting, meeting-to-close, dormant reactivation)
3. **Less manual coordination** - qualitative softener, use to pair with one of the two quantitative claims above

**Usage rules**:
- Every Tier A post must include at least one of these ranges where the context supports it.
- Cite as "directional based on AiiACo engagement observations" the first time each range appears in a post.
- Never inflate the range (e.g. do not say "5 to 10x" unless Nemr confirms a new range).
- Never stack the ranges in a way that implies compounding (e.g. do not write "30 to 70 percent faster AND 2 to 3x conversion at the same time" without context).

**Example phrasings**:

- "Operators who integrate AI into Follow Up Boss typically see 30 to 70 percent faster workflows on the coordination tasks that were eating agent time, directional based on AiiACo engagement observations."
- "A dormant database reactivation engagement usually produces a 2 to 3x conversion lift against the brokerage's prior baseline for cold contacts."
- "The point of the integration is less manual coordination, not more dashboards. That shows up as 30 to 70 percent faster workflows on specific tasks."

### 5. Zero AI tells
Follow `skills/content-quality.md` banned vocabulary list. In particular, banned for AiiACo blog:

**Banned words and phrases**:
- delve, delves, delving
- tapestry
- realm, realms
- leverage, leveraging (use "use")
- navigate, navigating (use "handle" or "manage")
- unleash, unleashes, unleashing
- bespoke (use "custom" or "tailored")
- landscape (metaphorical use, not literal real estate)
- intricate, intricacy
- multifaceted
- holistic
- vibrant (non-literal)
- embark, embarks, embarking
- paradigm shift
- game changer, game-changing
- testament, testament to
- foster, fostering
- myriad
- ever-evolving
- crucial, crucially
- pivotal
- critical (when "important" works)
- robust (unless technical term)
- cutting-edge
- state of the art
- seamless, seamlessly (unless literal)
- comprehensive (unless literal)
- utilize (use "use")
- facilitate (use "help" or "let")
- in today's fast-paced world
- in the ever-changing world of
- at the end of the day
- when it comes to
- it's worth noting that
- it's important to note

**Banned structural patterns**:
- Opening a post with "In today's digital landscape..."
- Any opener with "In the ever-evolving..."
- Three consecutive sentences that start with the same word
- Bullet list with parallel-structure same-length items (sounds AI-generated)
- Ending with "In conclusion" or "To summarize"
- Rhetorical question followed by immediate answer ("What makes AI different? The answer lies in...")

**Banned em-dash / en-dash**: Absolute. Use hyphens, commas, parentheses, or rewrite. This is a project hard rule, not a preference.

## 4 template types (rotation rule: never 3 consecutive same type)

### Type 1: Long-form pillar (2500-3500 words)
Used for category-defining posts, framework breakdowns, deep vertical playbooks.

Structure:
1. META header
2. Direct Answer block (40-80 words)
3. 2-3 paragraph lede (story-led or stakes-led)
4. H2 framework announcement
5. 5-7 H3 framework components (each with 2-3 paragraphs + 1 data point)
6. H2 "Why this matters" or "Why point tools fail"
7. H2 "How long does it take" (timeline transparency)
8. Callout (Operator signal / Compliance note / etc.)
9. H2 "Who builds it" or equivalent
10. Closing paragraph with CTA implicit (not hard sell)
11. FAQ section (6 Q/A pairs)
12. Article tags

Example: `posts/what-is-an-ai-revenue-system.html`

### Type 2: Short direct-answer how-to (1200-1800 words)
Used for specific integration walkthroughs, quick frameworks, definitional pieces.

Structure:
1. META header
2. Direct Answer block
3. 1-2 paragraph lede
4. H2 "Step 1" / "Step 2" / etc. (3-5 steps)
5. Each step: 1-2 paragraphs + 1 specific tool or API reference
6. Callout (Compliance note typical)
7. FAQ section (5 Q/A pairs)
8. Article tags

Example: `posts/how-to-integrate-ai-into-a-real-estate-crm.html`

### Type 3: Story-led opinion (1200-1800 words)
Used for Nemr's contrarian takes, pattern observations, mistake lists.

Structure:
1. META header
2. Direct Answer block
3. Lede: a specific story or observation
4. H2 "Mistake 1" / "Observation 1" / etc. (4-6 numbered arguments)
5. Each: 2 paragraphs, first is the pattern, second is the correction
6. H2 "What good looks like" or "What to do instead"
7. Internal link to related framework post
8. FAQ section (5 Q/A pairs)
9. Article tags

Example: `posts/real-estate-brokerages-ai-mistakes.html`

### Type 4: Data piece / comparison (1500-3000 words)
Used for best-of posts, tool comparisons, ROI breakdowns, case data.

Structure:
1. META header
2. Direct Answer block
3. 2-paragraph methodology explanation
4. Comparison table upfront (most important element)
5. H2 per row / criterion / insight (5-8 sections)
6. Each with specific data + callout
7. H2 "Methodology" (explain how the ranking was produced)
8. Close with recommendation by segment
9. FAQ section (5 Q/A pairs)
10. Article tags

## Sentence and paragraph rules

- **Paragraph length**: 2-4 sentences. Never 1-sentence paragraphs for emphasis more than once per post. Never paragraphs over 6 sentences.
- **Sentence length**: Vary. Target a mix of short (5-10 words), medium (15-25 words), and long (30-40 words). Monitor burstiness.
- **Voice**: Active voice in 90 percent of sentences. Passive OK for specific reasons (when the actor is irrelevant).
- **First word variety**: Do not start 3 paragraphs in a row with the same word. Do not start 3 sentences in a row with "The".
- **Numbers**: Spell out one to nine, numerals for 10+. Exception: specific tool versions, API paths, percentages, dollar amounts.
- **Acronyms**: Spell out on first use unless the acronym is more recognizable than the expansion (CRM, API, SaaS, FHA, TRID, QM are fine).

## Entity naming conventions

- **Brand**: AiiACo (A-i-i-A-C-o, capital C). Never "AiiAco" (lowercase c), "Aiiaco", "AIIACO", "AI&Co", etc. This is Nemr's established rule per Manus HEAD commit `7668adf` ("AiiACo = company/team, AiA = voice agent"). Use everywhere in marketing, body copy, schema @name fields, titles, bylines.
- **Voice agent**: AiA (not AiiA, not AiiACo) refers ONLY to the voice agent product. Never to the company.
- **Founder**: Nemr Hallak. First reference: "Nemr Hallak, founder of AiiACo". Subsequent: "Nemr".
- **Company**: "AiiACo" in marketing voice. "the firm" or "we" in rare cases.
- **Products**: always capitalize tool names correctly (Follow Up Boss, kvCORE, BoomTown, Lofty, Encompass, Salesforce, HubSpot, GoHighLevel, Guesty, Hostaway).
- **Regulations**: EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, Fair Housing Act (FHA), TRID, Qualified Mortgage (QM), CFPB.

## Commonly used callout labels

- **Compliance note** - Regulatory caveat
- **Math check** - ROI / unit economics breakdown
- **Build note** - Implementation detail
- **Counter-example** - Where the pattern breaks

## Post-level quality gate

Before commit + deploy, run this mental checklist:

1. Does the Direct Answer block stand alone as a valid answer to the primary keyword query? (AI Overview test)
2. Can a real broker / LO / PM identify at least 3 specific tools or APIs named in the post?
3. Is Nemr's voice present in at least 2 first-person passages?
4. Are there zero banned AI-tell words?
5. Are there zero em/en dashes?
6. Does the post cross-link to at least 1 aiiaco.com service page?
7. Does the FAQ section have 5+ pairs and would each answer stand alone as a Perplexity citation?
8. Is the template rotation obeyed (check last 2 posts before this one)?

If any check fails, fix before commit. No exceptions.

## Persona-specific voice adjustments

### When writing for Persona A (real estate broker)
- Name specific CRMs agents actually use (Follow Up Boss, kvCORE)
- Reference commission structures and agent retention economics
- Cite Inman, HousingWire, Tom Ferry as adjacent sources

### When writing for Persona B (mortgage ops director)
- Name specific LOS (Encompass, Black Knight)
- Reference compliance regimes (TRID, QM, CFPB)
- Cite MBA, HousingWire Mortgage, StratmorGroup as adjacent sources

### When writing for Persona C (vacation rental ops)
- Name specific PMSs (Guesty, Hostaway, Hospitable)
- Reference unit count economics (50-500 units is the sweet spot)
- Cite VRMA, Rental Scale-Up as adjacent sources

### When writing for Persona D (consulting partner)
- Reference proposal dynamics and competitive pressure from Big 4
- Use HBR and McKinsey as framing contrast
- Avoid consultant-jargon ("North Star", "value chain", "strategic imperative")
