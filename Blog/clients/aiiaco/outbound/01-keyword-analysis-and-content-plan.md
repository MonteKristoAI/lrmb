## What we learned in one page

1. **The positioning around AI revenue systems is defensible and almost entirely uncontested.** Only a handful of pages attempt to own the category and none of them do it with a clean definitional framework. There is a clear lane for aiiaco.com to become the reference page in search results and in AI answer engines for what an AI revenue system actually is.

2. **Your target buyers search for tools, not positioning terms.** Operators in real estate, mortgage, vacation rental, and boutique consulting do not search for "AI integration layer" or "enterprise AI infrastructure." They search for "AI for real estate agents", "AI for mortgage loan officers", "AI SDR", "AI consultants for small business", and "AI workflow automation." The plan below converts your positioning into content that meets those searches where they actually happen.

3. **Google is actively surfacing AI answers for almost every query on your content plane.** Nine of fifteen priority queries returned a Google AI Overview box in our live SERP scans. Every blog post we write needs to be structured so Google, Perplexity, and ChatGPT can cite its passages directly. That is a real shift from traditional SEO and it plays to AiiACo's strengths.

4. **Practitioner voice is almost entirely absent from the top rankings.** Big Four advisory firms, trade associations, and tool vendors own most of the SERP. None of them write in a founder voice with specific tools named, actual deployment sequences, or transparent outcomes. There is a visible gap for content that reads as if it were written by someone who has actually integrated AI into a Follow Up Boss or Encompass account.

5. **The four verticals reinforce each other.** Real estate, mortgage, vacation rental, and boutique consulting share a common operator profile: a mid-sized operation on an existing platform that needs AI on top without ripping out the platform. The blog content will bounce readers between the verticals via shared framework posts, so a mortgage operations director reading about dormant database reactivation will be one click away from the AiiACo story.

## How we built the keyword list

We seeded DataForSEO Labs with the twenty-five terms your ICP actually types into search, then expanded through related queries, deduped 618 raw candidates, and filtered for intent and commercial signal. We enriched the final set with live keyword difficulty, search volume, cost per click, and search intent. We ran live SERP checks on the top queries and captured the AI Overview presence, the People Also Ask questions, and the ranking domains. Every keyword in the tables below is a real term with a real monthly search volume in the US market.

We scored every keyword into one of three tiers:

- **Platinum**: low difficulty, meaningful monthly volume, commercial or informational intent. These are the clearest wins.
- **Gold**: moderate difficulty, meaningful volume. These are winnable with strong content once a few Platinum posts rank.
- **Silver**: higher difficulty, high volume. These are longer-horizon plays that reward patience.

The 60 keywords below are the curated list we propose as the anchor set for the AiiACo blog content plan.

## Proposed content direction

The plan below is a full content program anchored on the 10 clusters above. Every post has a primary keyword from the tables, a defined word count target, an internal linking strategy, and a direct answer passage designed to feed Google AI Overviews and Perplexity citations. We are not chasing volume. We are building depth in ten tightly defined clusters so that the blog compounds into a category-defining reference for AiiACo.

### Five anchor pillars we want to own

1. **What is an AI revenue system.** The category definition. This is the flagship pillar and the foundation of the content strategy. From it we extend with a rollout playbook, a measurement framework, and a build-versus-buy decision guide.

2. **How to integrate AI into the CRM you already run.** A series covering Follow Up Boss, kvCORE, BoomTown, Lofty, Salesforce, HubSpot, GoHighLevel, and Encompass. Each post walks through the actual integration pattern without requiring readers to switch platforms.

3. **AI for specific verticals the big firms ignore.** Real estate brokerages, mortgage loan officers, vacation rental operators with 50 to 500 units, and boutique consulting firms. Four verticals with dedicated pillar content and supporting how-tos.

4. **Dormant database reactivation as the highest-ROI AI use case.** This is the category you can own outright. One pillar on the mechanics, one post on the math, one post on the mortgage-specific version, one on the real estate version.

5. **AI governance and compliance for mid-market operators.** The EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, and ISO 42001 applied to real brokerages and lenders. This is the trust moat that Big Four advisory firms will not write for anyone under Fortune 500.

### Template variety

We plan to rotate between four content formats so the blog reads as a series written by a real operator, not a content farm:

- Long-form pillar posts that define a category and own a head term.
- Shorter how-to walkthroughs that show the integration step by step.
- Opinion posts in your voice that take a clear position on common industry mistakes.
- Data and comparison pieces that rank options and publish real performance numbers.

No two consecutive posts will use the same format.

### AI search and citation readiness

Every post will include a direct answer passage at the top, a framework or numbered list in the body, and a structured FAQ section at the bottom. These are the three passage formats that Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and ChatGPT most often cite. Every post will also be wired into the blog's existing BlogPosting, BreadcrumbList, and FAQPage JSON-LD schemas so answer engines can parse the content correctly.

### Voice

Every post will be written in your first-person operator voice, drawing on the positioning work we completed in the earlier rounds. The blog is an extension of your manifesto and your point of view, not a generic consulting blog. We will name specific tools, cite specific platforms, and publish real ranges of outcomes where we have them.
