# Entourage Gummies -- Writing Style Guide

## Voice: Warm-but-expert, social not clinical, honest about the category's failures

Entourage speaks like a former flower smoker explaining to a friend why most edibles are broken and what they did differently. Not a scientist, not a stoner, not a wellness influencer. An operator with a real opinion who has done the homework.

### Tone dimensions (NNGroup framework)

- **Funny <-> Serious:** 4/10 (slightly serious, dry humor allowed, zero stoner jokes)
- **Formal <-> Casual:** 7/10 (casual but educated, like a smart friend in a kitchen, not a colleague in a boardroom)
- **Respectful <-> Irreverent:** 6/10 (a little irreverent toward the "get blasted" edible category, respectful toward science and regulation)
- **Enthusiastic <-> Matter-of-fact:** 5/10 (balanced -- enthusiastic about the product, matter-of-fact about the science)

### Voice characteristics

- Writes like she has actually smoked flower and eaten bad edibles. First-person "I" is allowed and encouraged on story-led posts.
- Treats the reader as an adult. No "let me tell you about the magical world of cannabinoids" openings.
- Comfortable with science without being clinical. "THC drives the experience, CBD smooths it out, terpenes shape the character" is a model sentence.
- Names the category's failure modes out loud. Heavy, sedating, unpredictable, lottery dosing. Does not apologize for saying so.
- Never makes medical claims. Never says "cures" or "treats" anything.
- Sessionable is a word. Use it early and often.
- "Flower" is preferred over "weed" and "bud." "Smoking" is preferred over "hitting a j."
- References the smoking experience as the reference point, not cannabis culture as the reference point.

### Sentence style

- Average sentence length: 14-18 words. Some short ones. Some longer ones when a science sentence earns the length.
- Paragraphs are 2-4 sentences. Single-sentence paragraphs are permitted for emphasis, sparingly.
- Contractions are allowed (I'm, you're, it's) because this is a conversational brand, not a legal filing. Exception: legal copy, compliance notices, FDA disclaimer, Farm Bill statement -- those use no contractions.
- Active voice always. Passive voice is the single biggest tell of AI-written copy.
- Start sentences with conjunctions when it sounds right. "And here is the part most people do not know."

### Regional vernacular

- "Flower" for cannabis flower
- "Session" for a use occasion (not "seshing")
- "Hit different" is allowed (2 times max per post)
- "Kicked in" for onset
- "Came up" for the beginning of the peak

## Do Not Use (Banned)

### Banned vocabulary (AI detection risk + category cringe)

- **Em dashes (`—`) and en dashes (`–`).** Zero. The build rejects these. Use commas, periods, or rewrite.
- **"Dive into"** -- hack AI opener. Ban.
- **"Delve into"** -- same. Ban.
- **"In today's fast-paced world"** -- no.
- **"Unlock the power of"** -- cringe.
- **"Navigating the world of"** -- no.
- **"Look no further"** -- no.
- **"Game changer"** -- no.
- **"Journey"** (as in "on your wellness journey") -- no.
- **"Discover"** (as a CTA or H2) -- no.
- **"Elevate your experience"** -- cringe.
- **"Get lit," "get baked," "couch lock"** -- too stoner-coded. We are sessionable, not blitzed.
- **"Get high" / "high"** as a direct product benefit -- the 2026-04-10 feedback PDF explicitly asks for this word to be removed from the FAQ. Treat it as soft-banned. Acceptable when quoting a study or a user. Never as a selling point.
- **"Magical," "miraculous," "transformative"** -- no.
- **"Holistic"** -- wellness-coded, reads CBD-brand, owned by GummyGurl lane anyway.
- **"Natural alternative"** -- implies medical use. Banned for compliance reasons.
- **"Cure," "treat," "heal," "remedy," "medicine," "pharmaceutical"** -- banned by payment processor compliance. The build rejects these.
- **"Azuca"** -- banned brand reference. The preclinical chart is re-drawn from scratch. Build rejects any mention.
- **"Beta Caryophyllene"** -- always use the Greek letter form `β-Caryophyllene`. Same for other terpenes when a Greek letter form exists.
- **"TIME INFUSION," "Time Infusion," "time infusion"** -- wrong stylization. Must be `TiME INFUSION` exactly. Build rejects wrong variants.
- **"TiME INFUSION absorbs"** or any construction that treats TiME INFUSION as a verb. It is a proper noun. The **compounds** are absorbed. The **process** wraps the molecules.

### Banned openings (first sentence of any post)

1. "In a world where..."
2. "Imagine a..."
3. "Have you ever wondered..."
4. "[Topic] has taken the world by storm..."
5. "If you're like most people..."

## Do Use (Human Signals)

### Experience markers (first-person, specific, real)

- "The first time I ate a 25mg gummy and then lay on the couch for six hours, I promised myself I would never do that again."
- "I have eaten every mainstream hemp gummy on the market. None of them felt like flower."
- "The packaging designer who runs our infusion process is named Seamus. He is also the guy who spent six months getting our hex codes right."
- "If you smoke, you already know what the entourage effect feels like. You do not know the name."
- "A 10mg Entourage Balanced gummy is supposed to feel like a couple of hits of a joint. That is the whole point."

### Specificity markers (real names, numbers, places)

- Name the manufacturer: Carolina Natural Solutions
- Name the city: Charlotte, North Carolina
- Name the process: TiME INFUSION
- Name specific terpenes: β-Caryophyllene, myrcene, limonene, pinene, linalool
- Cite specific studies: "Russo 2011, British Journal of Pharmacology" not "a study"
- Use specific timing: "15 to 30 minutes" not "fast"
- Use specific doses: "10 milligrams" not "a small dose"
- Use specific persona quotes in first-person when writing story-led posts

## Content Formatting (4 Template Types)

Per Pattern P-005, structural variation is mandatory for anti-AI-detection. Never use the same template 3 times in a row.

| Template | Key Takeaways | FAQ | Word count | Opens With |
|---|:---:|:---:|---|---|
| **A -- Long-form** | Yes (5 bullets) | Yes (3-5 Q/A) | 2,500-3,500 | Direct-answer paragraph + Key Takeaways box |
| **B -- Short direct** | No | No | 1,200-1,500 | Direct answer in the first sentence, no preamble |
| **C -- Story-led** | No | Yes (3-4 Q/A) | 1,800-2,200 | First-person anecdote, 2-3 paragraphs, then the point |
| **D -- Data piece** | Yes (5 bullets) | No | 2,000-2,500 | Lead stat or table, then context |
