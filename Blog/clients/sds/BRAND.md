### The positioning in one paragraph
SDS builds wireline surface acquisition hardware and software for crews who don't want to be owned by a single-vendor ecosystem. Warrior connects to virtually any downhole tool from any manufacturer through one surface unit and one unified software interface. The 2026 flagship Warrior Universal Panel consolidates multiple legacy panels into a single hardware unit with job-specific interfaces and Auto-Perf firing. Manufactured and tested in Houston since 1993. Distributed worldwide — including by Weatherford, one of the industry's four largest wireline majors. Priced for switching with a $2,500/month lease program.

1. **Vendor-agnostic provably:** 60% open-hole tools, 85% cased-hole tools, 90% addressable switches across manufacturers
2. **Weatherford distributes us** — third-party proof no other competitor can claim

## Off-Limits Topics

- **Drilling (vs wireline).** Drilling is adjacent but not our audience. Mention drilling only as context.
- **Downstream refining, pipelines, midstream.** Not our audience.
- **Political commentary on oil & gas policy.** Stay technical. No climate or political takes.
- **Vendor bashing.** Criticism of competitors must be data-driven, never personal. Never say "SLB's approach is bad" — say "SLB's MAXIS system is architected around SLB's own tool ecosystem, which limits cross-manufacturer flexibility."
- **Price quoting for specific products.** Warrior leasing at $2,500/month is publicly announced and fair game. Competitor pricing is NOT public and must never be guessed or invented.
- **Company anecdotes without permission.** No customer names, no operator names, no named operator quotes without explicit written permission from the named party.
- **Claims about features Warrior doesn't have.** No "tractor-conveyed direct connect", no "high-power tractor interface", no auto-setup — these are planned but NOT shipping.
- **Marketing speak about the brand.** "Our mission", "our commitment", "our values" — out. The blog is technical, not inspirational.
- **Any medical, health, or safety ADVICE** that could create liability. Cite BSEE/API/OSHA for regulatory claims; never write prescriptive safety advice.

## Legal / Compliance Notes

### Safety disclaimer (mandatory on posts discussing perforating, well control, or regulatory compliance)
> **Note:** This post is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal, regulatory, or operational safety advice. Consult API Recommended Practice 67, BSEE guidance, OSHA 29 CFR 1910.109, and your company's safety management system before making operational decisions.

### No regulatory claims without citation
Never state that something "complies with API RP 67" or "is BSEE-approved" without citing the specific document or regulatory finding.

### Named quotes require permission
Any quote attributed to a real person (Christopher Knight, customer, SPE author) requires explicit written permission. Default to paraphrased quotes from published sources instead.

### No medical claims
Even in safety contexts, avoid claims like "this prevents injury" or "this protects crews." Instead cite specific safety test data: "DynaStage is RF-safe at 4 GHz and 300 V/m, exceeding API RP 67 requirements."

### Export control awareness
Some wireline technology is dual-use / export-controlled. Do not publish technical specifications that could violate ITAR or EAR. When in doubt, leave it out.

## Brand Voice vs Blog Voice

**The SDS 2026 Brand Voice Manifesto v4** defines the overall SDS brand voice. The blog voice is a **practitioner subset** of it — more field-operative, less corporate-identity-pillars. The blog voice:
- Keeps the manifesto's 5 identity pillars (Rugged Tech, Modern Wisdom, Verifiable Integrity, Culture, Stewardship of Resources)
- Drops the website's aspirational headlines ("Digital precision for a physical world" is a tagline, not a blog opener)
- Focuses on what the reader has to do today, not what SDS aspires to be
