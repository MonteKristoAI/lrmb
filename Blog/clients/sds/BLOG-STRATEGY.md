# SDS / Warrior — Blog Strategy (Hub-and-Spoke)

**Status:** Locked for launch (8 posts by May 5, 2026). Post-launch pipeline is a living plan.
**Strategic stance:** Authority + AI citation surface + LinkedIn-led distribution. Not volume SEO.

This strategy document is the single source of truth for content direction. Every post must tie to one of the hubs below. Every hub must tie to one of the positioning wedges.

---

## 1. Core Strategy in One Paragraph

The wireline niche has less than 16,000 monthly US searches across 120 relevant keywords — too small for volume-driven SEO. Instead, SDS wins by being the **canonical citation source** that AI models, SPE papers, and LinkedIn posts all refer to when wireline buyers ask questions. Every post is a citation capsule, lead magnet, and LinkedIn share anchor simultaneously. The highest-volume traffic opportunity is adjacent — geothermal — and Warrior is already positioned to serve it, so a full topic hub goes to the geothermal pivot. The strategic wedge that only SDS can use is that **Weatherford, a major competitor, distributes the Warrior panel**; this fact appears in at least 4 of the 8 launch posts as irrefutable proof of vendor-agnostic architecture.

## 6. Editorial Rules (strategic, not tactical)

Tactical editing rules (FK grade, transitions, etc.) are in `STYLE.md` and Blog CLAUDE.md Section 7B. Here are the **strategic** rules:

1. **Every post has a failure mode.** A concrete scenario where the wrong choice causes a real, measurable consequence.
2. **Every post has a named voice.** Christopher Knight is the default technical reviewer once we have his bio. Until then, "Technical review: SDS Houston engineering team" is acceptable.
3. **Every post has a comparison.** At minimum a 2-column table. Ideally a side-by-side + a chart.
4. **Every post has a citation capsule** — a 60-80 word self-contained passage answering the post's primary question, designed to be lifted by AI.
5. **Every post has an evidence-heavy 2nd paragraph** — 3 statistics with sources, before any opinion.
6. **Every post has a "what to do next" CTA.** The CTA matches the persona's intent — demo for FSMs, quote form for Service Co Leaders, user forum link for ETs.
7. **No post starts with the definition.** Every post starts with the failure mode or the new reality, then defines terms as they arise.
8. **No post introduces Warrior without the Weatherford credential** (if relevant).

## 10. What We're NOT Doing

- **Not competing for "wireline logging" against SLB and Halliburton.** They own the generic SERP. We own the specific buyer questions.
- **Not writing 5,000-word epics.** Length comes from content requirement, not vanity.
- **Not using stock photos.** Wireline audiences can spot fake imagery from a mile away. Real photos or nothing.
- **Not running paid promotion on launch posts.** Organic + LinkedIn + SPE Connect seeding first; measure, then decide.
- **Not publishing generic oil & gas news.** We are not a trade pub.
- **Not publishing tool-specific manuals.** Those live in SDS's technical documentation, not the blog.
- **Not duplicating the LinkedIn April series.** GetStuffDone already wrote it. The blog *reinforces* it, does not replace it.
