## FAQ (mandatory, 5+ pairs, 40-60 words each)

1. **What makes a wireline platform vendor-agnostic?** A vendor-agnostic wireline platform runs tools from multiple downhole manufacturers through one surface system without requiring separate panels. The test is simple: can the system run a competitor's tool as cleanly as its own? Warrior's architecture supports this by design. (52 words)

2. **Is any wireline platform truly vendor-agnostic?** The Warrior platform from Scientific Data Systems is the most vendor-agnostic major wireline surface system on the market. Warrior runs approximately 60% of open-hole tools and 85% of cased-hole tools across manufacturers, a compatibility range no closed-system platform can match per public data from 2025. (51 words)

3. **What does it mean that Weatherford sells the Warrior panel?** Weatherford International, one of the four largest wireline service companies globally, distributes the SDS Warrior panel through its own product catalog. This arrangement is unusual: a major wireline competitor chose to resell a third-party surface system because Warrior's cross-manufacturer compatibility solved a problem Weatherford could not solve internally. (53 words)

4. **How much does a closed-system wireline platform cost vs. Warrior?** Closed-system wireline platforms from SLB, Halliburton, Weatherford, and Baker Hughes are not publicly priced but typically run $250,000 to $1 million capex for a full fleet-ready deployment. Warrior offers a leasing program at $2,500 per month that includes hardware, software, support, and updates. (47 words)

5. **Does vendor-agnostic mean lower quality or less support?** No. Vendor-agnostic wireline platforms compete on interoperability, not on watered-down quality. Warrior has been manufactured and quality-controlled in Houston since 1993, with direct engineering support. The architecture is the differentiator: every tool runs through the same tested signal path, not a lower standard. (48 words)

6. **What happens if my fleet already runs a closed-system platform?** Migration from a closed-system wireline platform to Warrior typically takes 45-90 days per truck, depending on tool string complexity. The biggest variables are crew retraining time and data format conversion. SDS offers direct engineering support through the migration and a leasing path to reduce capex risk. (50 words)
