## FAQ (5+)

1. **What is API RP 67?** API RP 67 is the American Petroleum Institute's Recommended Practice for Oilfield Explosives Safety. First published in 1994 and most recently revised in 2015, it governs how perforating explosives are handled, transported, stored, and armed on oilfield operations. It is the de facto compliance baseline for wireline perforating crews in the United States.

2. **When is the 4th edition expected to publish?** The 4th edition of API RP 67 has been in development since at least 2017 per a published SPE Annual Technical Conference paper (SPE-187206-MS). A 2026 publication is the current expectation, though API does not publish firm schedules in advance. Monitor api.org and SPE announcements for the official release.

3. **What's the biggest change in the 4th edition?** The most significant change introduced in the 4th edition is strict RF-distance arming rules. Detonators must be armed at specified minimum distances from RF sources. In practice, this makes RF-safe addressable firing systems effectively mandatory for offshore operations and preferable for onshore operations in areas with RF equipment.

4. **Does my current perforating system comply with the 4th edition?** That depends on your addressable switch and detonator family. DynaEnergetics' DynaStage is RF-safe per the company's published safety data (4 GHz, 300 V/m). Hunting Titan ControlFire has RF-safe variants. Legacy non-addressable or non-RF-safe systems may require replacement or modification when the 4th edition publishes.

5. **How long does a wireline crew typically need to come into compliance?** API revisions typically have a 6–18 month industry adoption window. During this window, crews must update their equipment, retrain personnel, revise Job Safety Analysis forms, and document compliance. Early action (6 months before the deadline) is cheaper than last-minute compliance.

6. **Does the Warrior Universal Panel help with API RP 67 compliance?** The Warrior Universal Panel runs the three major RF-safe addressable switch families (DynaStage, Titan ControlFire RF-safe, G&H DSS) through a single interface. Auto-Perf mode sequences the firing cycle with documented safety margins. The panel's unified diagnostic output supports compliance audit documentation.
