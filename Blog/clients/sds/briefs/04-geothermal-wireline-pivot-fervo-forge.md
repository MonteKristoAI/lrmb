## FAQ (6 pairs)

1. **What is the geothermal wireline pivot?** The geothermal wireline pivot describes the migration of oil and gas wireline service capabilities to enhanced geothermal systems (EGS) and ultradeep geothermal wells. It's driven by DOE-funded projects like Utah FORGE, private EGS projects from Fervo Energy, and growing demand from data center power agreements. Wireline contractors with versatile surface systems can add geothermal as a revenue line.

2. **How does Fervo Energy use wireline services?** Fervo Energy uses wireline services for formation evaluation, fracture mapping, and production logging in enhanced geothermal wells. Their 2025 Sugarloaf appraisal well reached 520°F at 15,765 feet in 16 days, which required high-temperature logging tools and surface systems capable of capturing fiber optic distributed temperature sensing (DTS) and distributed acoustic sensing (DAS) data.

3. **What is Utah FORGE?** Utah FORGE is the U.S. Department of Energy's Frontier Observatory for Research in Geothermal Energy — a $300 million research initiative launched in 2014 to create enhanced geothermal reservoirs where none exist naturally. Between October 2020 and April 2024, the FORGE team drilled two first-of-their-kind EGS wells and performed multiple stimulations.

4. **Can the Warrior wireline platform run geothermal jobs?** Yes. According to Scientific Data Systems' public platform description, Warrior serves oil and gas, geothermal, water well, environmental, and mining operations worldwide. The same surface acquisition interface that runs cased-hole and open-hole logging in oil wells runs geothermal logging and reservoir monitoring. The downhole tool temperature limits are separate — the surface system runs at ambient.

5. **What temperatures do geothermal wireline tools need to handle?** Enhanced geothermal wells targeted by DOE FORGE Topic 1 funding require tools operational at 225°C for up to 12 months. Conventional oil and gas wireline tools typically handle 150–175°C. This temperature gap is the biggest engineering challenge in the geothermal wireline pivot, and DOE research is actively funding high-temperature tool development.

6. **Is geothermal wireline going to replace oil and gas wireline?** No. The two markets will coexist for decades. Oil and gas wireline remains the dominant revenue source and will grow at 3-7% CAGR through 2032 per multiple analyst forecasts. Geothermal wireline is an emerging high-growth sub-market driven by EGS project expansion, data center power demand, and DOE funding. Versatile wireline contractors can serve both.
