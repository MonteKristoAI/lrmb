## FAQ (5+)

1. **Does Weatherford really sell the SDS Warrior panel?** Yes. Weatherford International lists the Scientific Data Systems Warrior Well Logging System Panel in its own product catalog under wireline surface equipment. The listing names SDS as the OEM and describes the panel's cross-manufacturer tool compatibility. The URL is public: weatherford.com/products-and-services/drilling-and-evaluation/wireline-products/telemetry-systems/scientific-data-systems-warrior-well-logging-system-panel.

2. **Why would a wireline major distribute a competitor's panel?** Weatherford's decision to carry Warrior reflects what operators asked for: cross-manufacturer tool compatibility that Weatherford could not deliver through a proprietary panel. The distribution arrangement is commercial — Warrior solves a problem for Weatherford's wireline customers that Weatherford's own tools, running on a proprietary panel, could not solve alone.

3. **Do SLB, Halliburton, or Baker Hughes also sell the Warrior panel?** No. Among the four largest wireline service companies, only Weatherford distributes SDS Warrior. SLB sells its MAXIS system, Halliburton sells InSite, and Baker Hughes sells CIRRUS — each architected around its own downhole tool ecosystem. Warrior is the only third-party wireline surface system carried in a major's catalog.

4. **Is the Weatherford-SDS arrangement a partnership or a reseller agreement?** Based on public documentation, Weatherford distributes the Warrior panel through its product catalog. The arrangement is commercial and does not appear to include co-branding or a deeper technical partnership. Scientific Data Systems manufactures the Warrior panel in Houston and supplies it to multiple global distributors including Weatherford.

5. **Does this mean Warrior is objectively better than the major proprietary systems?** No single wireline surface platform is objectively better in every scenario. Warrior's advantage is cross-manufacturer compatibility, which matters most when operators mix tool manufacturers on a single job. Proprietary systems have advantages in vendor-locked environments where a single manufacturer's tools are used exclusively. The Weatherford distribution arrangement indicates that enough operators prefer the cross-manufacturer option for Weatherford to maintain it.

6. **How can operators use this fact when specifying wireline services?** Operators specifying wireline services for a well can require a vendor-agnostic surface system as a contract clause. Citing the Weatherford catalog as evidence makes the specification defensible. For multi-tool jobs, the Warrior platform's cross-manufacturer compatibility is documented and verifiable through a major wireline company's own distribution channel.
