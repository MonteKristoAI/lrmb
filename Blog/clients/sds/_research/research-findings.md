## 1. Critical Finding: This is a Near-Zero-Volume Niche

The entire 120-keyword wireline universe has only **15,590 monthly searches in the US**. For context, one blog post about breathwork gets more volume than every wireline system keyword combined.

**Implications for blog strategy:**
1. **Volume-driven SEO does not work here.** We cannot rely on "rank for X keyword, get Y traffic".
2. **AI citation surface is the primary play.** ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews answer ~10× more wireline questions than organic Google search. If SDS becomes the canonical passage source, that's the game.
3. **Authority building beats keyword density.** Every post must be the objectively best answer to a question, even if only 20 people per month ask it.
4. **Lead magnets > organic traffic.** The ICP lives on LinkedIn, SPE forums, and industry publications. Blog content exists to feed those distribution channels and to gate high-intent lead capture.
5. **The real traffic opportunity is adjacent: geothermal.** See Section 3.

## 7. Wireline Data Standards (LAS / DLIS / WITSML)

### Historical foundation (from Wikipedia, KGS, Energistics)

| Standard | Year introduced | Stewardship | Format | Current use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **LAS 1.2** | 1989 | Canadian Well Logging Society | ASCII | Retired |
| **LAS 2.0** | Sept 1992 | CWLS | ASCII | **Dominant** in 2026, still default export |
| **LAS 3.0** | 1999 | CWLS | ASCII (richer) | Secondary, growing |
| **DLIS** | — | API RP 66 | Binary | Modern wireline + LWD, dynamic sampling |
| **LIS** | 1970s | — | Binary | Legacy |
| **BIT** | 1970s | — | Binary | Legacy |
| **WITSML** | 2000s | Energistics (SPE) | XML/web | Wellsite-to-office real-time transfer |

### Content wedge
There is no public comprehensive "LAS vs DLIS vs WITSML for wireline practitioners" guide. Drilling Contractor has a high-level piece. KGS has a technical PDF. Nobody has the field-crew-friendly version. This is a perfect **pillar post** for Warrior.

**Anchor stat:** "LAS 2.0, released in September 1992, remains the dominant export format for wireline logs in 2026 — 34 years after its release. Warrior supports LAS 2.0, LAS 3.0, and DLIS output natively." (Weave in — this matches SDS's own 34-year heritage.)

## 9. AI Citation Landscape (informed estimate — actual tested in CITATIONS.md)

Based on query patterns for this niche, expected AI citation behavior:

- **ChatGPT / GPT-4 class models** pull heavily from Wikipedia, SLB glossary pages, SPE/OnePetro abstracts, Drilling Contractor articles, and Rigzone tutorials for wireline explanations. Rarely cite vendor sites.
- **Perplexity** tends to cite specific product pages and manufacturer press releases when the query is commercial, and SPE/JPT when the query is technical.
- **Google AI Overview** pulls from the first 5-10 organic results when they exist, and from Wikipedia + featured snippet sources when they don't.

**What this means for SDS:**
- Every blog post should have a **Citation Capsule** — a 60-80 word self-contained passage that answers the post's primary question, designed to be lifted whole by AI. Structure: "What is X? X is [definition]. [Key mechanism]. [Key benefit]. [Vendor/sourcing note]."
- FAQ schema is mandatory. Google AI Overview explicitly prefers structured Q&A.
- Entity definitions must happen **at first mention** with a schema-friendly sentence. E.g., "The Warrior Universal Panel (ULPP) is a consolidated surface acquisition unit that runs multiple wireline switches, tool strings, and surface sensors through a single command interface."
- Cross-link aggressively to authoritative external sources (SPE, DOE, API, BSEE) so AI crawlers see the post sitting inside a credible neighborhood.

## 12. Strategy Implications (feeds BLOG-STRATEGY.md)

1. **Pivot content strategy around 3 hub-and-spokes:** (a) Vendor-Agnostic Wireline, (b) Perforating Automation Safety, (c) Geothermal Wireline Pivot. The third is where the volume actually lives.
2. **Make every post a citation capsule.** Optimize for AI pickup, not Google rank position.
3. **Use the Weatherford-distributes-Warrior fact as a signature positioning asset.** It shows up in at least 4 of the 8 launch posts.
4. **Target the API RP 67 4th edition regulatory moment.** This is the flagship launch post. It's a calendar-driven anchor that wireline buyers will search once the 4th edition drops — SDS should own the #1 answer to "What does API RP 67 4th ed mean for my operations?"
5. **Build a talent-pipeline sub-cluster.** "wireline career" and "wireline job" have real volume and SDS is a trusted brand name to oilfield people in Houston. A single post — "What a wireline engineer actually does (and how to become one)" — captures both audiences.
6. **Avoid publishing against the majors head-on.** We will never outrank slb.com for "wireline logging". Instead, target questions the majors can't answer because their business model prevents them (e.g., "What's the best wireline platform that runs every manufacturer's tools?").
