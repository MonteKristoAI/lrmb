Bridge Hospice - Site Architecture

Which page structure best serves Bridge Hospice's audiences? Their current site has 15 pages but poor organization. The new site needs clear paths for families, healthcare providers, and community supporters.

COMPREHENSIVE (12 pages)
Homepage (hero + all previews)
├── For Families/
│   ├── Services
│   ├── What to Expect (patient journey)
│   └── FAQs
├── For Providers/
│   └── Referral Portal
├── Programs/
│   ├── Valor (Veterans)
│   └── Dave's Wish (Nonprofit)
├── About (Story + Team + Awards)
├── Get Involved (Volunteer + Careers + Donate)
├── Blog
└── Contact

A. Comprehensive (12 pages)

Dedicated pages for each audience. "What to Expect" patient journey page. Separate referral portal for providers. Valor and Dave's Wish get their own spotlight pages. Most complete but most build effort.

FOCUSED (8 pages)
Homepage (hero + services + programs + testimonials + CTA)
├── Services (all services + "What to Expect" section)
├── Valor (Veterans program)
├── Dave's Wish (Nonprofit + Donate)
├── About (Story + Team + Awards + Gallery)
├── Referral (provider form)
├── Blog
└── Contact (form + volunteer + careers links)

B. Focused (8 pages) — RECOMMENDED

Every page earns its place. Valor and Dave's Wish get dedicated pages (they deserve it). Services includes "What to Expect". About consolidates story + team + gallery. Contact handles volunteer/careers. Clean, purposeful, buildable.

MINIMAL (5 pages)
Homepage (long-scroll: hero → services → programs → team → testimonials → FAQ → contact)
├── Valor (Veterans program)
├── Dave's Wish (Nonprofit)
├── About (Story + Team)
└── Blog

C. Minimal (5 pages)

Long-scroll homepage does the heavy lifting. Only Valor, Dave's Wish, About and Blog get separate pages. Fastest to build, simplest for users. Risk: homepage becomes too long, referral path is weak.

Key audiences: (1) Families exploring hospice for a loved one — need reassurance, clear info, easy contact. (2) Healthcare providers making referrals — need fast, frictionless referral form. (3) Community supporters — volunteers, donors (Dave's Wish), job seekers. The architecture must serve all three without overwhelming any.
