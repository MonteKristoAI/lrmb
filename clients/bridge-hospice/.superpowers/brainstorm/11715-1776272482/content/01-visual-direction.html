Bridge Hospice - Visual Direction

Which visual mood best represents Bridge Hospice's brand? Consider: this is a hospice serving families in their most vulnerable moments. The design must inspire trust, peace, and warmth.

Sanctuary
Forest Green + Warm Gold + Linen

A. "Sanctuary" - Nature & Warmth

Deep forest greens, warm golds, linen whites. Serif headings (Playfair Display). Feels like a peaceful garden retreat. Organic shapes, leaf motifs, generous whitespace. Photography: sunlit rooms, nature, gentle hands.

Strengths

  • Deeply calming and healing
  • Unique - stands apart from clinical healthcare
  • Connects to nature/peace metaphor
Harbor
Deep Blue + Soft Gold + Cream

B. "Harbor" - Trust & Dignity

Deep navy blues, soft gold accents, warm cream backgrounds. Modern serif + clean sans-serif (Lora + Inter). Feels like a trusted institution with heart. Subtle wave/bridge motifs. Photography: family moments, dignified portraits.

Strengths

  • Professional yet warm - perfect for healthcare
  • "Bridge" metaphor aligns with brand name
  • Navy conveys trust and stability
Embrace
Warm Neutrals + Terracotta + Sage

C. "Embrace" - Warm & Human

Warm neutrals, terracotta, soft sage green. Light, airy feel with abundant whitespace. Humanist fonts (DM Serif Display + DM Sans). Minimal, editorial aesthetic. Photography: intimate close-ups, warm light, real human connection.

Strengths

  • Most modern and editorial
  • Feels like a premium wellness brand
  • Warm earth tones = comfort and home

Context: Bridge Hospice is in Lubbock, TX. Their current site uses orange accents (feels like a construction company). They serve families facing end-of-life decisions - the design must feel safe, dignified, and warm, never clinical or corporate. Their brand name "Bridge" and key programs (Valor veterans, Dave's Wish nonprofit) offer strong storytelling opportunities.
