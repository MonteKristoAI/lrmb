Your Journey

What to Expect With Bridge Hospice

From the first phone call to ongoing bereavement support, we guide your family through every step with clarity, compassion, and respect.

1

Initial Consultation

Our care team visits your home within 24 hours. We listen, answer every question, and explain what hospice care looks like in practice.

2

Care Plan Development

Physician, nurse, social worker, and chaplain collaborate to build a personalized plan tailored to medical needs and family wishes.

3

Your Team Arrives

Within days, your interdisciplinary team begins regular visits. Medications, equipment, and supplies delivered directly to your home.

4

Ongoing Support

Consistent visit schedule with 24/7 on-call nursing. Immediate access to clinical guidance whenever you need it most.

5

Bereavement & Beyond

13 months of grief counseling, support groups, and memorial services. Our care continues long after the hardest day.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Hospice care is specialized medical care focused on providing comfort, pain relief, and emotional support to patients with a life-limiting illness. Rather than pursuing curative treatment, hospice prioritizes quality of life for both the patient and their family. Our interdisciplinary team includes physicians, nurses, social workers, chaplains, and trained volunteers who work together to address physical, emotional, and spiritual needs.
Hospice is appropriate when a physician determines that a patient has a life expectancy of six months or less if the illness follows its natural course. However, many families wish they had started hospice sooner. If your loved one is experiencing frequent hospitalizations, declining appetite, increased pain, or a general decline in daily functioning, it may be time to have a conversation with their doctor about hospice eligibility.
Yes. Medicare, Medicaid, and most private insurance plans cover hospice care at little to no cost to the patient or family. The Medicare Hospice Benefit covers physician services, nursing care, medications related to the terminal diagnosis, medical equipment, and counseling. Our admissions team handles all insurance verification so your family can focus on spending time together.
Absolutely. The majority of our patients receive care in their own homes, surrounded by the people and places they love most. We deliver all necessary medications, medical equipment, and supplies directly to you. Our team visits on a regular schedule and is available 24/7 by phone for urgent needs. We also provide hospice care in assisted living facilities and nursing homes throughout the Lubbock area.
Your care team includes a board-certified hospice physician, registered nurses, certified nursing assistants, a licensed social worker, a chaplain, trained volunteers, and a bereavement counselor. Each team member plays a specific role in managing your loved one's comfort and supporting your family. Our medical director, Dr. Larry Warmoth, personally oversees all patient care plans.
Anyone can make a referral, including family members, friends, physicians, social workers, and discharge planners. Simply call us at 806-993-3900 or fill out the consultation form on this page. A member of our admissions team will respond within 24 hours to discuss your loved one's needs and schedule an in-home evaluation at no cost.
