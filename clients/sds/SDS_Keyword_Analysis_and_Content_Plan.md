## Executive Summary

The wireline data acquisition space is uncommonly favorable for authority-building content. Across a 120-keyword research pass, 30 of the 42 commercially relevant keywords returned a keyword difficulty score at or below 20 on a 100-point scale. Not a single keyword in the entire set scored above 60. In plain terms, the search landscape is wide open for anyone willing to publish technically credible, field-validated content. The majors have not written the field-crew-facing guides this audience needs, and no independent voice has filled the gap.

The research surfaced a second finding that reshapes the strategy in a positive direction. The adjacent geothermal well logging market carries more than 14,800 monthly searches in the United States alone, and every flagship keyword in that cluster returned low competition. SDS already serves geothermal, water well, environmental, and mining operations. That means the Warrior blog can reach a much larger audience than the pure wireline services numbers would suggest, and every new reader is already inside the Warrior service envelope.

The third finding is the most strategic. Weatherford International lists the SDS Warrior panel inside its own product catalog under wireline surface equipment. That single piece of public evidence converts the vendor-agnostic positioning from a marketing statement into an engineering fact validated by a global wireline competitor. The content plan is built to put that fact in front of every buyer who asks whether vendor-agnostic wireline is real.

The keyword landscape, the content architecture, and the initial post direction are detailed below. Please review and share any adjustments you would like before we move into content creation.

No one writes for the crew running the job, and the crew running the job is exactly who buys and specifies a wireline surface system.

The search numbers confirm the audience. That is more addressable demand than the entire core wireline keyword set combined.

That single public fact is the strongest proof point available for any vendor-agnostic claim in the wireline space, and it belongs in the content plan as a centerpiece.

These three findings together define a plan that is technically credible, audience-first, and anchored in facts the audience can verify without taking the content on trust.

## Content Architecture

The blog is structured as five topic hubs. Each hub has a flagship pillar article that defines the category, and a set of spoke articles that expand on specific questions inside the category. Spokes link to the pillar, the pillar links to the spokes, and the whole hub-and-spoke structure signals to search engines, industry publications, and artificial intelligence systems that the Warrior blog is the authoritative voice on its topics.

### Hub A. The Vendor-Agnostic Wireline Platform

This hub addresses the architectural question at the center of the Warrior value proposition. The pillar article defines what a vendor-agnostic wireline platform actually is, contrasts it with closed-system surface platforms, and uses the Weatherford distribution credential as the central piece of verifiable evidence. Spokes extend the hub into compatibility coverage, the migration path from a proprietary system, and the questions a buyer should ask before selecting any wireline surface system.

### Hub B. The Warrior Universal Panel

This hub is the product-flagship cluster. The pillar article introduces the Warrior Universal Panel, explains the consolidation of multiple legacy surface panels into one hardware unit, and describes how the Universal Panel works with every major manufacturer's tools through a single standardized interface. Spokes cover the hardware architecture, the wireline truck consolidation math, crew training on standardized interfaces, and the relationship between the Universal Panel and Auto-Perf mode.

### Hub C. Perforating Automation and Addressable Switches

This hub addresses the perforating-side of the wireline audience. The pillar article is the definitive independent comparison of the three major addressable switch systems on the market, a comparison only the Warrior platform can credibly publish because Warrior runs all three. Spokes extend the hub into the operational economics of switch compatibility, the safety test data behind each major system, and the practical implications of the next edition of API Recommended Practice 67 for every perforating crew in the field.

### Hub D. The Geothermal Wireline Transition

This hub is the adjacent-market flagship and the largest volume opportunity in the plan. The pillar article explains the enhanced geothermal systems story, names the programs and operators driving it, and shows how the same Warrior platform that runs oilfield jobs also runs geothermal operations today. Spokes extend the hub into high-temperature tool considerations, the economics of adding geothermal as a service line, the Fervo and FORGE programs specifically, and the adjacent water well and environmental logging markets that the Warrior platform already supports.

### Hub E. Wireline Economics and the Path to Switching

This hub addresses the commercial and financial side of the wireline surface system decision. The pillar article explores the real cost math of running a wireline fleet, the total cost of ownership across a typical fleet lifecycle, and the leasing option as a way to avoid the upfront capital objection that keeps many service companies locked into older systems. Spokes extend the hub into the specific economics of switching from a proprietary system, the operational case studies that make the numbers concrete, and the strategic questions that a service company owner must answer before making any equipment decision.

## Editorial and Production Principles

Every article in the plan is written to a single quality standard, and every article must clear the same non-negotiable gates before it is published.

**Field-first voice.** The blog is written by the people who would actually be at the wellsite, for the people who would actually be at the wellsite. The prose is engineer-to-engineer. Nothing reads as marketing. Every operational detail is specific enough that a working crew recognizes it.

**Sourced claims.** Every statistic has a year, a named source, and a link the reader can click. Every named entity is defined the first time it appears. No generic assertions. No unsupported comparisons.

**Comparison over prose.** Every article includes at least one comparison table. Tables communicate structured differences far more clearly than paragraphs, and readers recall them longer.

**Failure modes over theory.** Every technical article includes at least one specific scenario where the wrong decision causes a real, operationally meaningful consequence. Abstract theory does not land with this audience. Concrete situations do.

**The Weatherford credential in context.** The Weatherford product catalog listing for the Warrior panel is the strongest single piece of public evidence the plan has access to. It appears where it is relevant, as evidence, not as a sales line.

**Respect for the reader's expertise.** The audience has already seen every marketing pitch in the oilfield. The blog does not try to impress them. The blog tries to be genuinely useful to them.

**A zero-tolerance rule for the phrasing this audience dislikes.** The SDS 2026 Brand Voice Manifesto v4 is the canonical guide for language choices, and it is followed to the letter. The blog uses "universally compatible" rather than any more abstract term. The blog uses "standardized interfaces" rather than any phrase that would imply setup automation the platform does not perform. The blog uses "field-validated" rather than any language that could suggest equipment is old or behind the times. These choices are intentional and absolute.

## Measurement Philosophy

The blog's success is measured along three lines.

**First, authority signals.** The count of artificial intelligence citations, the number of external references to Warrior blog content in other trade publications and social platforms, and the depth of coverage across each hub. When a wireline engineer asks an artificial intelligence assistant what a universal wireline panel is, the answer that comes back should cite the Warrior blog.

**Second, qualified engagement.** The number of visitors who spend meaningful time on the articles that matter most to their role. The number of visitors who follow the content all the way to the frequently asked questions section. The number of readers who reach the call-to-action and take the next step. Quality of engagement matters more than raw traffic in a niche this technical.

**Third, direct commercial outcomes.** The quote form submissions attributable to blog content. The demo requests. The inbound questions from operators exploring the geothermal pivot. The service company leaders who surface a Warrior conversation with their teams because they read the vendor-agnostic architecture article. These are the numbers that justify the entire effort.

These measurements are reported on a simple rhythm. Share of voice on the authority signals, growth on the engagement signals, and a monthly summary of the commercial conversations the content has influenced.
