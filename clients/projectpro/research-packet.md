## 2. ICP Persona: The Building Material Sales Leader

### Primary ICP
**Title:** VP of Sales / Regional Sales Manager / Director of Sales
**Company:** Commercial building material supplier, distributor, or manufacturer (50-500 employees)
**Industry verticals:** Roofing, windows/doors, siding/facades, insulation, lumber, lighting, HVAC, stone/masonry, steel framing, concrete products

### Daily Reality (synthesized from multiple sources)

**Morning:**
- Reviews pipeline in CRM, sees mostly stale opportunities
- Gets email alerts from Dodge/ConstructConnect with projects already in bid phase
- Assigns reps to call on new leads - but most are already "spoken for" by competitors who showed up during design

**Throughout the day:**
- Manages a team of 5-15 outside sales reps covering a multi-state territory
- Reps spend 2-3 hours per day sourcing leads manually: scanning bid boards, checking plan rooms, calling GC contacts for tips
- Reps cold call architects who don't pick up (architects get cold called by hundreds of manufacturers)
- Reps show up at bid stage and compete on price alone against 5-10 other suppliers
- Deals with lost specs - product was substituted because competitor got to the architect 3 months earlier

**Recurring frustrations:**
- Paying $6,000-$15,000/year for Dodge or ConstructConnect and still finding projects too late
- Reps are reactive, not proactive - they chase bid invitations instead of influencing specifications
- No systematic way to find projects in planning/design phase
- When they do find early-stage projects, the contact info is incomplete or outdated
- Sales cycle is long and unpredictable - no consistent weekly pipeline of new opportunities
- Junior reps lack industry relationships and have no way to get in front of architects early

**End of quarter:**
- Pipeline has gaps because reps spent too much time chasing projects they were never going to win
- Lost deals where "we had the better product but they went with whoever was already in the spec"
- Board pressure to grow revenue without adding headcount

### Secondary ICP
**Title:** Outside Sales Rep / Territory Manager / Architectural Sales Rep
**Company:** Same as above, but they are the individual users opening the Monday list
**Daily reality:** Driving between jobsites, lunch-and-learns with architects, trade shows, cold calling from the truck. They need a ready-to-call list, not a database to search.

## 4. Competitor Creative Analysis

### Dodge Construction Network
**Positioning:** "Construction Industry Data, News & Project Intelligence" - positions as a comprehensive data platform
**Pricing:** $6,000/year per seat (regional), up to $12,000+/year (national), enterprise $40,000-$75,000/yr for 5+ seats
**Key weakness exposed in research:**
- 49 BBB complaints in 3 years (20 in last 12 months)
- Aggressive auto-renewal contracts, non-cancellable terms
- Projects surface too late (bid phase, not planning phase)
- Database-oriented - you search for projects, nobody finds you
- Overpriced for small/mid suppliers

**Dodge vs. ProjectPro positioning gap:** Dodge is $6K-$15K/year for a database you search yourself. ProjectPro is $199-$399/month for a curated, contact-verified Excel list delivered every Monday. Dodge requires training and daily database work. ProjectPro requires opening an email.

### ConstructConnect
**Positioning:** "Finding and Winning Projects" - preconstruction workflow platform
**Pricing:** $4,800/year entry, $36,000-$42,000/year for 5-person team with national access
**Key weakness exposed in research:**
- Shallower early-stage intelligence - captures projects primarily at bid-phase
- 31% of users encounter outdated/inaccurate info monthly
- Clunky UI complaints
- Expensive for small businesses
- ContractorTalk users say they "didn't get anything out of CC"

**ConstructConnect vs. ProjectPro positioning gap:** CC is a platform with dashboards, CRM, takeoff tools - complexity that smaller suppliers don't need. ProjectPro strips it to what matters: verified contacts, early-stage projects, Excel, Monday delivery.

## 6. Emotional Triggers

### Fears
- **"I'm always the last to know."** Projects appear on their radar after specs are written. The fear of missing the window is constant.
- **"We have the better product but keep losing to whoever got there first."** Product quality becomes irrelevant when you're late.
- **"I'm paying thousands a year for a database that gives me the same leads every competitor gets."** The fear that expensive tools deliver no competitive advantage.
- **"My reps are busy but not productive."** Activity without outcomes. Lots of calls, few specs won.
- **"We're one bad quarter from layoffs."** Revenue gaps caused by unpredictable pipeline.

### Frustrations
- **"Every Monday is a scramble."** No system, no list, no structure. Reps showing up asking "what should I work on today?"
- **"We're competing on price because we missed the only window that matters."** Margin erosion from late engagement.
- **"Dodge locked me into a contract for leads I can't use."** Contractual traps with non-cancellable terms.
- **"I cold called 50 architects this week. One picked up."** The futility of outbound when timing is wrong.
- **"Our CRM is full of dead projects."** Stale data from bid boards and databases.

### Aspirations
- **"I want my reps opening a ready-to-call list every Monday morning."** Predictability. Structure. No scrambling.
- **"I want to be the first call an architect gets, not the fifteenth bid response."** Being early means being chosen.
- **"I want to influence specs, not compete on price."** Shifting from commodity pricing to preferred vendor status.
- **"I want a sales system that works whether my best rep stays or goes."** Process over personality.
- **"I want to know about a $42M mixed-use project in Phoenix while the architect is still choosing materials."** Specific, tangible, desirable intelligence.
