ProjectPro replaces the scramble with a system. No dashboards, no logins, just a spreadsheet and a phone.

### CTA Button
Get Your First Monday List

### WHY

The first 125 characters ("Monday morning, 9:07am. Your rep opens an email, downloads a spreadsheet, and starts dialing. By 9:15, she is on the phone") work because of radical specificity. The time stamp (9:07am, not "Monday morning"), the exact actions (opens email, downloads spreadsheet, starts dialing), and the use of "your rep" make this feel like a scene from the reader's own office. The connection principle fires hard here: the VP of Sales is watching their own team in this description, except the team is performing the way they wish it actually performed.

Future pacing works by describing the transformed state as current fact, not as a promise. The copy never says "imagine if" or "what if." It states what happens as though it is already true. The contrast section ("here is what most teams do instead") then snaps the reader back to their painful present: 2-3 hours a day sourcing, architects who do not pick up, same projects as every competitor. The gap between the future state and the present state creates urgency to close that gap. The 11-minute pipeline meeting is the single most aspirational detail, because every sales leader has sat through a 45-minute Monday meeting where reps had nothing to report.
