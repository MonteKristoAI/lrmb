## 4. Campaign Performance by Iteration

### 4.1 V1-V3: Broad Creative Testing (March 10 - April 3)

This phase tested a wide range of creative angles across 30 unique ad concepts (each in 3 ad sets) to identify which messaging themes resonated with the ICP.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Spend | $871.75 CAD |
| Reach | 6,270 |
| Impressions | 11,447 |
| Link clicks | 429 |
| CTR | 3.75% |
| CPM | $76.16 |
| Conversions | 2 initiate_checkout |

**Key learnings from V1-V3:**

The broad testing surface generated valuable signal on which messaging themes drove engagement. Top performers by click volume:

| Creative | Clicks | CTR | Angle |
|----------|--------|-----|-------|
| Img 13 "Stop Selling Price, Sell Spec" | 52 | 19.19% | Spec positioning |
| Img 29 "Stop Walking Into Price Wars" | 58 | 6.29% | Margin protection |
| Img 25 "Your Window Before Bid" | 51 | 1.97% | Timing/spec window |
| Img 20 "Reps Are Chasing Ghosts" | 12 | 22.22% | Rep efficiency |
| Img 1 "After Bid" | 15 | 17.44% | Loss aversion |

These engagement patterns directly informed the V5 copy angles -- loss aversion, timing urgency, and spec window messaging all traced back to the strongest CTR signals in V1-V3.

The conversion rate was limited by the off-platform funnel (Meta ad to landing page to website to checkout). This was identified as the primary constraint and led to the pivot to native lead forms.

### 4.3 V5: Native Lead Form Campaign (April 5 - April 13)

The defining pivot of the engagement. We moved to native Meta lead forms, eliminating all off-platform friction, and deployed new copy built around the emotional triggers identified in V1-V4 testing.

**What changed in V5:**

| Element | V1-V4 | V5 |
|---------|-------|----|
| Conversion point | Website (off-platform) | Native Meta lead form |
| Steps to convert | 5 (click, load, navigate, select, checkout) | 1 (fill form) |
| Credit card | Required immediately | Not mentioned (SDR handles later) |
| Copy approach | Benefit-focused | Emotion-driven (loss aversion, embarrassment) |
| Targeting | Detailed targeting | Advantage+ Audience (broader, lower CPM) |
| Form type | N/A | "More Volume" (optimized for data collection) |
| Lead qualification | None | Gating question + lead tiering |

**V5 Per-Ad Performance:**

| Ad | Emotional Hook | Spend | Impressions | Clicks | Leads | CPL |
|----|---------------|-------|-------------|--------|-------|-----|
| AD A "They Showed Up First" | Loss Aversion | $102.85 | 1,105 | 21 | 5 | **$20.57** |
| AD D "Close The Google Tab" | Embarrassment / Waste | $501.01 | 6,860 | 76 | 13 | **$38.54** |
| AD B "Pipeline Dead" | Anxiety / Urgency | $6.30 | 58 | 0 | 0 | - |
| AD C "Ghost Data" | Frustration | $1.31 | 18 | 0 | 0 | - |
| **Total** | | **$611.47** | **8,041** | **97** | **18** | **$33.97** |

**AD A "They Showed Up First"** achieved the lowest CPL of the entire engagement at $20.57. The loss aversion angle -- "your competitor is already talking to the architect while you don't know the project exists" -- resonated strongly. Form completion rate of ~24% (5 leads from 21 clicks) is excellent for B2B.

**AD D "Close The Google Tab"** generated the highest volume with 13 leads. Meta's algorithm allocated 82% of total budget to this ad, finding a broad audience responsive to the embarrassment/waste angle. CPM of $73.03 was the lowest in the set.

**AD B and AD C** received minimal algorithmic allocation ($6.30 and $1.31 respectively) and were effectively untested. This is normal behavior for Meta's Andromeda system at this budget tier -- it concentrates spend on predicted winners.

**V4 pixel ads continued running alongside V5** and produced 3 additional pixel leads at significantly higher CPLs ($67-$178), further confirming the native form advantage.

## 6. What Worked & What We Learned

### Validated Decisions

| Decision | Result | Evidence |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Native lead form (vs website funnel) | 12.8x CPL improvement | $435 (website) vs $34 (native) |
| Loss aversion copy ("They showed up first") | Lowest CPL in engagement | $20.57, 24% form completion |
| Embarrassment angle ("Close the Google tab") | Highest lead volume | 13 leads, broadest reach |
| Creative consolidation (5 ads vs 30) | Faster algorithmic learning | V4 CPL 43% lower than V1-V3 |
| Advantage+ Audience (broad targeting) | Lower CPM, wider reach | V5 CPM $76 with strong conversion |
| Gating question in lead form | Lead quality segmentation | GOLD/SILVER/BRONZE tiering |
| Pain-specific copy over generic benefits | Significantly higher conversion | Spec window, timing, cost angles |
| Dave K testimonial integration | Strong social proof element | 28% open rate as proof point |

### Optimization Opportunities Identified

| Area | Finding | Recommended Action |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| Budget tier | $70/day keeps campaign in permanent Learning phase (need 50 conv/week, generating ~14) | Scale to $100-150/day for faster optimization |
| AD B & AD C | Insufficient data to evaluate (< $8 combined spend) | Rotate new test creative into these slots |
| Cold email channel | Designed but not yet launched | Activate Apollo + Instantly for parallel volume |
| SDR response time | 15-minute SLA designed but not formally activated | Confirm Kreel's availability and commitment |
| Video creative | Not tested yet | Produce 30-60s video (strongest format for B2B social) |
| Retargeting | Website visitors not retargeted | Create custom audience from site visitors |
| Seasonality | April is post-peak for commercial construction planning | Consider Jan-Mar window for next major push |
