Pipeline Inspection Technology for CCUS: Preparing Legacy Infrastructure

By EV Technical Team February 28, 2026 8 min read

Carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) is no longer a theoretical exercise. Major projects are progressing from front-end engineering through construction, and governments on both sides of the Atlantic are committing significant capital to CCUS infrastructure. A central component of that infrastructure is the pipeline network required to transport captured CO2 from emission sources to storage sites.

The economics of CCUS depend, in large part, on whether operators can repurpose existing pipeline infrastructure rather than build entirely new systems. Thousands of kilometers of legacy hydrocarbon pipelines are being evaluated for conversion to CO2 service. The critical question for every one of those pipelines is the same: what condition is it actually in?

The Challenge of Repurposing Legacy Pipelines

CO2 transport presents different demands than natural gas or crude oil. Dense-phase CO2 is highly corrosive in the presence of moisture. It operates at pressures that can exceed those of the pipeline's original design basis. And the regulatory framework for CO2 pipelines is evolving rapidly, with inspection requirements that may exceed those applied during the pipeline's original operating life.

Before any legacy pipeline can be reclassified for CO2 service, operators need comprehensive internal condition data. External surveys (aerial, satellite, ground-based) cannot assess internal wall condition, coating integrity, or the presence of debris and deposits. Traditional inline inspection (ILI) using magnetic flux leakage or ultrasonic tools provides wall thickness data but cannot visualize surface conditions, weld quality from the inside, or the nature of deposits and corrosion products.

This is where visual pipeline inspection fills a gap that other technologies leave open.

PigCAM Technology for Pipeline Assessment

EV's PigCAM systems are free-swimming, self-contained pipeline camera platforms designed for internal visual inspection of pipelines from 4 inches in diameter upward. Unlike tethered camera systems that are limited by cable length and deployment logistics, PigCAM units travel through the pipeline under flow or gravity, recording high-definition video from multiple camera positions simultaneously.

The PigCAM 4 carries four cameras providing 360-degree coverage and operates for up to 24 hours on a single deployment. For larger diameter pipelines, the PigCAM 8 carries eight cameras, delivers full circumferential coverage, and operates for up to 96 hours with a range of 176 kilometers. Both systems record continuously, producing a complete visual record of the pipeline's internal condition from launch to retrieval.

For CCUS pipeline assessments, this visual record is invaluable. Operators can identify:

  • Internal corrosion patterns. The distribution, morphology, and apparent severity of corrosion across the full length of the inspected segment.
  • Coating condition. Whether internal coatings (where present) are intact, disbonded, blistered, or absent, and in which locations.
  • Debris and deposits. Sand, scale, wax, or other accumulations that could affect flow assurance or indicate ongoing degradation.
  • Weld condition. Visual assessment of girth welds, seam welds, and repair patches from the internal surface.
  • Mechanical damage. Dents, buckles, ovality, and other geometric anomalies visible from inside the pipe.

The INLINE Speed Control Partnership

EV's partnership with INLINE Speed Control adds a critical dimension to PigCAM deployments. Speed control technology ensures that the PigCAM unit travels through the pipeline at a consistent, optimal velocity regardless of pipeline profile or flow conditions. This consistency is essential for video quality: too fast, and the footage is blurred and unusable; too slow, and the deployment window is consumed before the full pipeline length is covered.

For long-distance CCUS pipeline assessments, where a single deployment may need to cover tens of kilometers, speed control transforms PigCAM from a useful tool into a reliable inspection solution. The combination of extended runtime, speed regulation, and multi-camera coverage means that operators receive comprehensive visual data for the entire pipeline segment in a single run.

Regulatory Considerations

The regulatory landscape for CO2 pipelines is tightening. In the United States, the PIPES Act and subsequent rulemaking from PHMSA are establishing inspection and integrity management requirements specific to CO2 transport. In Europe, the CCS Directive and national transposition legislation are creating comparable frameworks.

Visual inspection data from PigCAM deployments provides evidence that complements and enhances data from other ILI technologies. When regulators ask whether a pipeline is fit for CO2 service, the combination of wall thickness data (from MFL or UT tools) and internal surface condition data (from PigCAM) provides a substantially more complete answer than either dataset alone.

EV's AIVA platform can store, manage, and report on PigCAM data alongside other inspection datasets, creating a single digital record for each pipeline that supports regulatory submissions and ongoing integrity management.

Building the CCUS Pipeline of the Future

The CCUS infrastructure buildout is accelerating. Whether operators are converting existing pipelines or constructing new ones, visual inspection will play an expanding role in both pre-service qualification and ongoing integrity assurance. PigCAM technology is already deployed in CCUS pipeline assessments across multiple geographies, and the operational experience base is growing with each project.

For operators evaluating legacy infrastructure for CO2 service, the question is straightforward: do you have sufficient data to make a conversion decision? If the answer involves uncertainty about internal conditions, visual inspection through PigCAM provides the evidence needed to move forward with confidence.
