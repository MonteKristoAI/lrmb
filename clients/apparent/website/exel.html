How It Works

Three Components. One Complete System.

The eXeL system combines a stainless steel storage tank, dedicated PV solar panels, and our patented low-voltage DC heating assembly into a single, grid-independent water heating solution.

1

Stainless Steel Storage Tank

Available in 65-gallon and 105-gallon capacities. Premium stainless steel construction ensures longevity, corrosion resistance, and superior heat retention compared to conventional glass-lined tanks.

2

PV Solar Panel Array

Dedicated photovoltaic panels provide direct power to the heating system. These panels are not shared with your home electrical system, ensuring consistent hot water production regardless of household electricity demand.

3

Patented Low-Voltage DC Heating Assembly

The core of what makes eXeL different. Our patented DC heating element operates at low voltage, eliminating the need for inverters or grid connection. This is what allows the system to function during power outages.

Frequently Asked Questions

eXeL uses dedicated photovoltaic solar panels that connect directly to a patented low-voltage DC heating element inside the tank. Because the system operates on DC power at low voltage, it does not need an inverter or grid connection. Sunlight hits the panels, electricity flows directly to the heating element, and your water heats up.

The stainless steel tank provides excellent heat retention, keeping water hot for extended periods. On cloudy days, the PV panels still produce energy (at reduced output), and the system continues heating. For overnight use, the insulated tank maintains temperature, and a backup electric element can be configured for extended low-solar periods.

Traditional solar thermal systems circulate fluid through roof-mounted collectors, requiring pumps, glycol, expansion tanks, and complex plumbing. eXeL uses standard PV panels and a DC heating element, resulting in a much simpler installation, fewer failure points, and better cold-climate performance since there is no fluid that can freeze in the roof collectors.

The 65-gallon model is designed for households of 2-3 people and comes with a 2-panel PV array. The 105-gallon model serves 4+ person households with a 4-panel array for higher daily output. Contact our team for a sizing consultation based on your specific hot water usage patterns.

The eXeL system uses solar PV technology and may qualify for federal and state solar energy incentives, including the Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC). Eligibility varies by state and jurisdiction. We recommend consulting with your tax advisor and checking local incentive programs for the most current information.
